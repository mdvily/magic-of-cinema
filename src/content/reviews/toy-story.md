# **Toy Story (1995)**. After four years, Toy Story finally came into theaters in 1995, and became one of the most acclaimed ever animated features, as well as the first ever movie with CGI involved. Before this, animation was strictly confined to 2D, but this movie fixed that, with an IMDb rating of 8.3, a Rotten Tomato perfect 100%, and 96% with MetaCritic. But after 30 years and 8 months, does this film hold up? SPOILERS!

# Story

# I guess it is safe to say, that I have got a friend in the story of this film. The pacing is quite fast, yet the movie never takes advantage of it, making it too fast for my taste, as I feel that we get plenty of time with the story and every scene that the film takes place in. The story is unpredictable, as you never know what literal toys will get themselves into. However, my favorite aspect of the film is how it perfectly puts in hilarious comedy scenes and dramatic heartfelt and powerful segments. VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# The film was the first ever film to have CGI, and I thought it stood the test of time. You have no necessity to love the film’s character animation, or animation, period, but I for one, do. I feel like the character animation perfectly matches how normal how humans move, as the modeling is very fluid. In my opinion, the color as well, taking the shades of every color of the rainbow, and how it perfectly shows the fun and family and creativity aspects of the picture. The action scene in the end is visceral, as well as perfect for Woody accepting Buzz and Lightyear accepting that he is a toy. The character designs, finally, perfectly represent the creativity of this film, as prior to watching, you never know what the character designs will look like. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Let us begin with our main protagonist, Sheriff Woody. Woody is a jerk for part of the movie, but this still makes him likable, due to Woody having honor for many of the other toys, and in the end, he grows that honor and respect for Buzz Lightyear, in a slow, but earned character arc. Speaking of which, Buzz is also an iconic character. Buzz Lightyear passionately believes that he is a space ranger, but the film later reveals to him that he is a toy. I love how he still refuses to give up on Woody, making him an incredible role model to kids, who are being mistreated: to do something, but not go too far. Andy Davis is a loving owner to Woody, being the sole main reason on where the heart comes from. This makes me love even more Sid Phillips as our antagonist, with his bullying to toys and his younger sister, Hannah, making him the perfect foil and mirror to Andy. My only real gripe is that a lot of the cast, that being Andy’s toys, do feel like plot devices, as the film could have leant into more what they thought of Woody’s action. But if you can get pact that, the character list is very well done. VERDICT: 18/20

# Acting

# Tim Allen is Buzz Lightyear, and Allen really sells Lightyear’s beliefs that he indeed is not a toy, but an actual Space Ranger, and that the other toys are trying to pull a prank on him. The toys are portrayed by, Wallace Shawn, Annie Potts, the late Jim Varney, John Ratzenberger, and the lates Estelle Harris and Don Rickles are Woody’s iconic toy group, and their distinct personalities make their world feel lived in. John Morris is the owner of the Toys’ actor, Andrew “Andy” Davis, with his charm and skill at conveying emotions. Erik von Detten as Sid Phillips is weird, but with his character’s foolishness, he makes it work. However, I doubt that you can really talk about the cast of Toy Story (1995) without mentioning Tom Hanks as Sherrif Woody, with his emotions, line deliveries, everything. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# Randall “Randy” Newman was the man behind the film’s score, and I doubt that he could have done even a smidge better. I really only have two things to discuss about the music, but they are significant. First off, “Friend In Me” is iconic, because, A: It has the same beat as the main theme, coincidentally, and B: It narratively works in the film’s favor. Also, the score itself is iconic and perfectly works for the characters and the story, perfectly affecting the writing in the best possible way. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, Toy Story (1995) in my opinion, is a timeless classic, that still holds up today. I feel like the filmmakers really wanted to do something special with this film, and I also get the feeling that it worked so well, because I hereby approve of this film. I would recommend the film to any avid person who either likes toys, or PIXAR animation studios. Final Score: 98%
