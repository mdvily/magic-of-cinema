# **Tarzan II (2005)**. After the 1999 masterpiece that came out 6 years ago, let us face it a prequel was bound to happen. But what happened during the prequel’s production? Well, since Alex D. Linz as Young Tarzan was grown up, Harrison Chad was replaced as him. Speaking of the casting changes, George Carlin was hired to portray Zugor. Anyways, the movie is considered to this day as not a great movie, but one of the studio’s better direct-to-dvd sequels. But is it really so? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# Man, the story just makes no sense. Tarzan wants to prove himself as an ape, he falls away from the apes to Dark Mountain, and meets the Zugor, only to learn maybe he is not so bad. Do you know how generic that is? The film never really does anything special with its premise, and that makes it generic. What I many times about a story is that it tries hard to set itself apart. That just is not the case here. There are also so many plot holes to the story. How does falling away from the apes get Tarzan closer to Dark Mountain? Why were Kago, Ugo, and Mama Gunda even in the story in the first place? Why does everyone think Zugor as a beast? Yes, the comedy is extremely funny for me, but how much does help the story be great? The comedy is very underutilized for me. The heart though, is the worst aspect of the movie. I never cried at any of the characters being kind to each other, and that is because of how mean-spirited the movie is. I always felt that the story never cared about its own existence, and consequently, nor could I. **VERDICT: 6/20**

# **Animation**

# Out of the 5 aspects of this movie, I find the animation the greatest aspect of the movie. My only real gripe is that the character designs are just plain ugly. They never feel like they look any different from what is exactly not their personalities. For example, Zugor looks very creepy, yet that is not in his personality. But get past that, and I loved the character animation. The characters move way more fluidly, than what you might expect from a direct to DVD sequel. They do not have the best track record, so this was a pleasant surprise. I also love the colors. The happy scenes are green, the sad scenes are blue, and the dark scenes are grey. This perfectly affects the scenes. But the lighting is the best aspect. The film lights the scene so well. It works, because it helps to welcome the viewer into the movie. **VERDICT: 16/20**

# **Characters**

# It is with great sadness that I must tell you, we are back to the bad aspects of the movie. Tarzan, our protagonist, is the odd one out from the gorillas. While it can be compelling for the film to tackle what the 1999 movie did not do all the time, the problem is the execution. Tarzan gets himself into trouble, for doing such stupid antics. It was his own fault that bad things happened to him, so I did not root for him that much. Kala and Kerchak are the catalysts to the story. While I love how they have different personalities, I must also factor in how they are very important to the story. Well, at least way more important than Terk and Tantor, because, what did they really do in the story? I feel like they just came into the movie, because they were important to Tarzan’s life in the first act of the movie released six years before this one. Now, onto the new characters! Mama Gunda, Kato and Uto are the film’s “villains”. I say that word loosely, because they never drive a conflict, or even do anything that bad. All they do is goof around. Lastly, Zugor, while he did have an excellent portrayal from George Carlin, he could have been more than just a villain revealed to not be a villain. I feel like the film did the bare minimum with that, and he could have had more story purpose. **VERDICT: 8/20**

# **Acting**

# Harrison Chad is Tarzan. Chad is charming and a bit charismatic. He really is, but whenever his character is fear, he never supports it. Glenn Close and Lance Henriksen are Kala and Kerchak. Close has plenty of great chemistry with Tarzan, and I love how Henriksen has a bitterness in his voice. This works, because it makes him understand his character. Brenda Grate and Harrison Fahn are Terk and Tantor. The two of them have such poor chemistry, because they always sound like they argue with each other. This does not work, because that was never the case, in the first place. Ron Perlman and Brad Garrett are Kato and Ugo. Perlman and Garrett have such terrible chemistry, but at least they sound stupid, just like their characters. Finally, my second favorite part of the movie is George Carlin as Zugor. Carlin has such a grumpy voice. This perfectly showcases his character, but his chemistry with Chad does not disappoint. **VERDICT: 15/20**

# **Music**

# The music does not exactly slap, but if I called it less than good, I would be lying about my opinion. "Son of Man" (Reprise) is Tarzan trying to master the ways of the jungle. The good is that this was very appropriate for the movie. The bad is that they already did that in the original. I wish that they had more original stuff in the scene, and the song is a part of that. "Leaving Home (Find My Way)" is Tarzan leaving the gorilla family. It sucks, because of how predictable the song is. Of course they were going to put that in the movie. "Who Am I?" is the greatest piece of music in the movie. It perfectly represents Tarzan’s character arc. **VERDICT: 10/20**

# **In conclusion**, Tarzan II (2005) is definitely not my biggest cup of tea. I found the writing to be pretty bad, and the music to be mixed. However, with well done animation, and pretty surprisingly good voice acting, I will say that this could have been worse. If you are a die hard fan of the original, then you might want to would check it out, but if not, then maybe skip this one. **Final Score: 55%**
