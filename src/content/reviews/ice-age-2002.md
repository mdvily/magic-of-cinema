# **Ice Age (2002)**. For Blue Sky’s first ever film, Ice Age remains the potentially most acclaimed film the studio has ever put out. The crew wanted to make this similar to The Land Before Time (1986), but with a more lighthearted tone. The result? A film that fans refer to as a great film that is considered more powerful than you may think. But is it to be? SPOILERS!

# Story

# At a first glance, the story seems every flavor of basic, and even generic at times. The film is merely only a Point A to Point B trope, without much difference from the other chains of the dynasty. However, is it about the story itself, or what is inside of the story, which gives it spice, brings it to life, gives it a vibe? For me, it is entirely what the story is made of, and for me, the greatest aspect of the picture is the comedy, supplying the film with a lot of laughs. And you know what? It clearly works! Yes, there is quite a bit of gross-out and slapstick humor from Sid, but the comedy is way better than one may think at first glance, as on second glance, I come to appreciate the dialogue and banter between Manny, Sid and Diego. I love how the film perfectly enhances how the obstacles, that the characters must embrace as well. You never know how the characters will survive on their own, making for some fascinating character development. Yet, the heart is the most glorious aspect of the movie, as the film keeps using powerful moments, such as the cave segment, where we find out that Manny’s wife and son were brutally murdered, and I love how the film focuses more on showing and not telling, a golden cinema rule, when you come to think about it. VERDICT: 16/20

# Animation

# The film looks absolutely gorgeous, with my only criticism being that the human designs could be improved upon. But, get past the one criticism, and you hereby leave yourself with an animated marvel. For the first act of the movie, the film wraps itself around colors red and pink, but when the characters enter the snowy biome, the film gets way more beautiful, perfectly reflecting the time period. Even the lush red and pink colors look beautiful, reflecting the future banter and potential of the movie. The animal character designs and animation is extremely accurate to how the prehistoric animals moved at looked like. But my favorite part of the animation is undeniably, without an icy doubt, is the action scenes. The action scenes deliver upon so much sheer intensity, and the characters doing what they do for these scenes really goes the action-and its choreography-gives it an unsaturated, complete, vibe. VERDICT: 18/20

# Characters

# Why not begin with our min Mammoth, Manny? Manny starts off as a grumpy, lonely mammoth, due to his wife and son having been killed, but by the end, he ends up as a kind mammoth, who knows the true meaning of kindness. Our immature Sid the Sloth goes from being stupid and annoying, to being more responsible. Diego goes from being a determined hunter and killer, to being a better tiger, who learns the true meaning of how a heard sticks together, no matter what. Scrat is our comic relief, and while I wish he had more story significance, he does have his fair share of humorous antics. Finally, I wish we could have seen our antagonist, Soto more, but he is a threat with a motive you understand, even if you do not agree with, in the first place. To conclude, the movie has very little characters, and with the sequels, that is unequivocally for the better. VERDICT: 18/20

# Acting

# Ray Romano is Manny, and Romano really delivers as Manfried, in terms of witty one-liners, as well as emotion, but is not afraid to let the silence speak for itself when need be. John Leguizamo is Sid, and he delivers on both the jokes, as well as the heartfelt moments, like where he mourns Diego’s temporary death. Denis Leary is Diego, and at the start, Leary knows how to instill fear into his character, as well as being more soft-toned by the end. Tara Sands is Baby Roshan, and she delivers on the adorable moments, and can convey such emotion, without even using any words, just baby talk. Christopher Wedge has his comedic moments at Scrat, and can get a laugh out of me in every scene that Wedge’s Scrat stars in. Goran Visnjic is the villain, Soto, and  Visnjic knows exactly how to deliver the fear, vendetta and wrath all at once into his character, without sounding over the top. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# This film has musical magic, in a way other blue sky movies just cannot match, to the point, where it is the greatest aspect of the film. First off, there is a main theme, such as the some of the  scenes, but there is also a comedy tracks where stupid things happen, such as when the three change Roshan’s diaper, deciding who should do this disgusting task. However, my favorite part of the music is “Send Me On My Way” by Rusted Root, as it is a song about embarking on a journey, exactly what the four are doing! VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, I cannot help but really enjoy Blue Sky’s very first ever animated feature and love Ice Age (2002) so much! Ice Age is a powerful film about opening up to the world that you live in, and becoming a better person, all while having a lot of humorous comedy. I would factually recommend this film to anyone and everyone who enjoys a good comedy. Final Score: 92%
