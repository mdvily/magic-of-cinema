# The Sound Of Music. For Robert Wise’s 28th film, he made it about Maria, a high-spirited novice nun who becomes governess to the seven children of a widowed naval captain. Through music and love, she heals the family, marries the Captain, and helps them escape the Nazi annexation of Austria. This film is arguably Wise’s second most acclaimed movie he has ever directed, right behind West Side Story (1961). It is considered one of the most acclaimed and uplifting movies of all time. But is it so? SPOILERS!

# Story

# The story here is wonderful. First off, I love how the funny comedy is balanced out with a powerful sense of drama and emotional impact. Also, the story is unpredictable. You never know what is going to happen, due to smartly-timed subversion. Due to this being a musical, about a nun becoming a stepmother, you may occasionally forget about the Nazi’s. Also, the story establishes the characters so well, it is astounding to me. The film takes its time to show us who these characters are. To conclude, the story is unique and charming, yet has an incredibly warm hearted tone. VERDICT: 20/20

# Visuals

# The colors look beautiful, mostly sticking to shades of purple and light blue. They perfectly hit the lovely tone of the movie. The clothing the characters wear perfectly describes, by showing rather than telling the personality of the characters. Lastly, I love every camera movement in the movie, because it perfectly establishes what is going on in the story. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Maria Rainer is our heroine. She is an incredible role-model to women, especially mothers, with her kindness and encouragement. Captain Georg von Trapp is Maria’s husband, by the end. He is a strict father to his 5 daughters and 2 sons. However, across the film, he learns to be more kindhearted. For the children, you have Liesl: the eldest, who is blossoming into a young woman and falls in love with Rolf. Friedrich: the eldest boy, who tries to act tough.Louisa: Age 13, known for her rebellious and mischievous streak. Kurt: gentle, playful, and loves to sing. Brigitta: smart, observant, and an avid reader. Marta: sweet-natured and gentle, and Gretl: The youngest, soft-spoken but incredibly sweet. I love how every child has a different personality. The Mother Abbess, Max Detweiler and Baroness Elsa Schraeder are perfect for the film’s warmhearted tone, while having their own character traits. Rolf Gruber is a perfect character, in terms of moral decay, due to you believing he is going to be a good person. Lastly, Herr Zeller is an incredibly fearful villain. To conclude, this movie is incredible, in terms of character. VERDICT: 20/20

# Acting

# The acting performances of the 60’s were consistently amazing, so I had high expectations for the film’s cast. And sure enough, they exceeded! Julie Andrews as Maria Rainer is iconically spirited, hopeful and warm. Christopher Plummer as Baron Von Trapp is stern, yet has a soft side for good people, like Maria. Charmian Carr, Nicholas Hammond, Heather Menzies, Angela Cartwright, Debbie Turner and Kym Karath are Maria and Baron’s kids, and they perfectly convey emotion into the seven kiddos. Peggy Wood as Mother Abbess is kind and loving. Finally, Ben Wright as Hans Zeller is menacing, and fearful. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# With “Music” in the picture’s title, the film must sound good. And sure enough, this has one of the best scores and songs in the history of the seventh art! The score is perfect, yet it is the songs that are incredibly close to being the greatest ever! "Preludium" / "Morning Hymn" / "Alleluia" is perfect for the film’s tone. "The Sound of Music" is nothing short of iconic. Its lyrics, vocals and purpose cannot be improved upon. "Maria" is perfect for establishing Maria’s personality. "I Have Confidence" is uplifting and warm and "Sixteen Going on Seventeen" has a lot of purpose for an aspect of Liesl’s character. "My Favorite Things" and "Do-Re-Mi" are songs I learned in chorus, for a reason: they are iconic. However, "The Lonely Goatherd" and "Edelweiss", along with "So Long, Farewell" bring the needed fun into the film. But If you want to be even more iconic, "Climb Ev'ry Mountain", "Something Good" have you covered. Lastly, the songs go out with a bang with "Processional and Maria". I do not know why I had chills, as I first heard it, but I know I did. VERDICT: 20/20

# In conclusion, there is a reason that I find the story, visuals, characters, acting and music so incredible. I saw the film in theaters in December, for its 60th-year-anniversary. The film, in my personal opinion has so much effort put into it, that even six decades later, it has stood the test of time. Final Score: 100%
