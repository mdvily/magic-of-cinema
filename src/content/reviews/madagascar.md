# Madagascar. Dreamworks was basically the 2000 decade Looney Tunes. Yes, they had quality, but this was normally found in Disney, or Pixar. However, if you wanted a good laugh, the boy in the moon was your jam. Shrek movies 1 and 2 started the shtick, but Madagascar exploded into that trope. But does that mean that it’s a masterful experience? SPOILERS!

# Story

# The story tells itself the same way that Marty thinks his is. We start off in the zoo, where Marty wants to be free, in the wild. But he gets that wish granted, in the country of Madagascar, home to the lemurs community. Now, the premise of “Person wants to be free, gets wish granted, stuff happens,” has been presented much of the time, and the film does not really do many things to make it different, except for how it has a lot of energy. The story also near-never shows anything and mostly tells us with annoying exposition. However, it is not about the story itself, but what is in the story. In the film, you will find humorous comedy, that ages well. Yes, there indeed is slapstick, but that gives the film flavor. However, the film still is willing to slow down for some heartfelt moments: such as when Marty talks to Alex before the climax, or when Alex apologizes to Marty. This is a funny movie, but it presents plenty of heart. VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# My personal favorite part of the picture is the animation. It was the first visual style from a 3D animation movie, to have the visual slapstick of a cartoon. I would say that it ages highly well. The colors are mostly shades of orange and green. The character animations and designs are perfect for the characters’ personalities, and the action of the climax works. My only critique, the human designs look so bad, you could argue they are but even finishedM VERDICT: 19/20

# Characters

# Now, this is a character-driven picture, that works. Alex is the funny, larger than life Lion. However, he has a prominent heart for others, like how he stands up to Howard, for making fun of Marty. Also, the premise of him possibly being the villain, due to his large appetite and animalistic nature. Our protagonist Marty is the same thing, however he is not remotely the villain, wants more than what he has, which is freedom, and has even more laughs. Gloria is the motherly figure, wanting the other 3 to do the right thing. Melman is funny, but his desire to not be  sick with germs does not add much to the overall plot. The penguins are essential to the story, due to them giving Marty his idea. Mason and Phil are barely here, but they do their job fine. The fossas are the antagonists of the film, and they are presented as terrifying, however, should not they be funny? The film has no understanding of what to do with them as villains. However, it is the lemurs that steal the show, in terms of both comedy and the plot. VERDICT: 18/20

# Acting

# Ben Stiller is Alex. He puts the high energy into this Lion, but can take himself more seriously, making his portrayal grounded. Marty is played by Chris Rock. Rock puts in a solid amount of emotion, kindness and humor. Jada Pinkett-Smith as Gloria has an appropriate maternal voice to the Lion, Zebra and Giraffe. Speaking of which, David Schwimmer is Melman, and while that was a great choice, he doesn’t really lend his voice. That, and he’s more excited than scared, and in this film, Melman was known for being scared. Also, Cedric The Entertainer as Maurice just doesn’t want to be in the picture. He did fine, but he has had better roles. However, Andy Richter as Mort was adorable. Fun fact: the penguins were played by part of the crew of the film. Director Tom McGrath is Skipper, story artist Chris Miller is Kowalski, assistant editor Christopher Knights is Private, and Jeffery Katzenberg is Rico himself. McGrath also played the Fossa. I hated it. It is so creepy, and it is supposed to be humorous. However, if you think of Sacha Baron Cohen, you think of King Julien III from this movie. VERDICT: 17/20

# Music

# The score is pretty good, fitting the scene nicely. There are multiple beats, such as: The main theme. The theme here is quite delightful, and captures the tone of the scene well. The panic mode track is a jam to listen to, and the highlight of hearing the film. You have, “I Like To Move It, Move It”, King Julien’s theme song, with amazing vocals from Baron Cohen, but not the best lyrics. However, I hated the fossa theme, because it thinks that the fossas are scary, however, they are supposed to be humorous. VERDICT: 16/20

# In conclusion, Madagascar (2005), is a film that is slowly aging. However, this film is still a very well done film. It is merely just like catching up with old friends, for a good laugh. Final Score: 85%
