**Lilo And Stitch (2002)**. The decade of the 2000’s can be summed up in one word: inconsistent. Yes, some movies at the time were done well, but others just fell flat, such as Chicken Little (2005), and Home On The Range (2004). Not to mention, how these movies mostly flopped hard at the box office! Lilo and Stitch, though, became an acclaimed Disney feature, in a time when we thought it was no longer possible! This movie was considered a diamond in the rough, and today, we are going to figure out why! **SPOILERS!**

**Story**

You might find two stories in this picture, for very good reason. You have Stitch’s story, where as a galactic criminal, He seeks to escape Jumba and Pleakely, via hiding as a dog. The other story, you have Lilo Pelekai, and her older sister Nani, trying to keep custody of her, due to them not having their parents. My only real criticism is that in order to balance the tones, the writers had to more than they actually did. For Stitch’s story, you have a cat and mouse chase, which gets comedic, and the drama between the two sisters. The tone gets pretty inconsistent, to the point where some may get tonal whiplash! But the plots themselves? Well, it depends on which story you talk about. The stitch plot line gets on average a chuckle out of me. Yes, some jokes were hilarious, but others just… fell flat, with the slapstick just not being my taste. The sister plot line however, is where the heart and drama come together, in order to form something truly special. As sisters, they fight a lot, but they still love and would do whatever they can for one another. I have younger siblings, so this is extremely accurate, and resonating with me. This presents the theme: family. This causes Nani to want Lilo taken care of and Lilo to want Stitch to behave well. Speaking of which, Stitch is also a driving force for the heart, as he starts to understand what the meaning of “Ohana” is, figuring out what is missing in his own life. Yes, the story can be broken, but it still works, because of how much it resonates with me. **VERDICT: 18/20**

**Animation**

Out of all of Disney’s movies, Lilo And Stitch stands out as one of the films that looks the most unique. Director Christopher “Chris” Sanders is well-known for what he typically does, and this applies here: to make everything out of roundness, elimination sharp corners that occasionally take away from the friendliness and welcoming of the movie, into its world. This applies heavily with the character designs, especially doing on to those of the aliens. This adds on to the creativity, making it look… otherworldly. And with the backgrounds, the otherworldly factor does not stop there. It still looks as advanced as the aliens want it to. But the character animation? It is amazing with the humans, but bordering on perfect, with the character animation of the aliens. They really show off their abilities, and the movement lets you find out for yourself. And as expected, the creativity works to an insane degree, because of this. But on Hawaii, it feels more familiar and artistic. Especially the human character designs, which look like they were made by artists, with how accurate they are to real life humans. It is even accurate on how they portray fire dancing, surfing and hula dancing! Finally, the film has a vibe of watercolor I knew it had in it, all this while. The blue represents the Hawaii setting and the meaning of family and love. Even the GGI looks pretty good! It feels like the aliens made this, and it adds on to the action so well. **VERDICT: 20/20**

**Characters**

As a movie about family, the writers had to make all of the characters feel like an actual family, an understandably tough kingdom to conquer. There also is another aspect on how the loss of parents affects the sisters mentally. Lilo is a girl who learns a lot how to be better behaved in the world around her, and with her trauma at such a young age, you get exactly why. Nani, the older sister of Lilo has challenges where you know why she gets so annoyed: With Stitch, it is hard for her to keep her job, Lilo can be a chore to babysit, and Bubbles is always on her case! One wrong move, and she no longer has her younger sister, Lilo. And then he was created by Dr. Jookimba, in order to destroy. But along the journey, he slowly learns how to be a better creature andd to do better deeds. Dr. Jumba and Pleakely are the comic reliefs, and while they do have an arc, their comedy is… mixed. The main three are the only three I love, the others are kind of one-dimensional. **VERDICT: 14/20**

**Acting**

The late Daveigh Chase is Lilo Pelekai. Chase knows exactly how to get into Lilo’s head, while sounding so cute, you buy her love for Stitch, and more affection to Nani by the end. Chris Sanders is Stitch 626, and Sanders sounds like a blue, havoc-causing alien from space, but he has so much charm and emotional depth. Tia Carrere is Nani Pelekai, who has such affection and chemistry to Chase’s Lilo, I thought that they were actual sisters! Amy Hill and Jason Scott Lee are Mrs. Hasagawa and Mr. Kawena and they do a pretty good job at delivering their stern side, as well as their warm and loving side for family. David Ogden Stiers and Kevin McDonald are the henchmen turned sidekicks Jumba and Pleakely, who have such affection vibe together, and you buy their silly banter. And now, the villain actors are Kevin Michael Richardson and the late Zoe Caldwell are Gantu and The Grand Councilwoman, who have such a presence, though, Richardson has a more uncanny voice. This did not really fit in his character, or the movie. **VERDICT: 19/20**

**Music**

The music is just incredible here, as it really sounds like it is from Hawaii. First off are the movie’s songs, and speaking of Hawaii, “Hawaiian Roller Coaster Ride” is the epitome of fun, and it would play well at Disneyland! "He Mele No Lilo" is our introduction to Lilo, and is bursting with life. You really buy how this song introduces us to Lilo, in such a fun way. Then, you have the Alan Silvestri tracks of “I'm Lost”, “You Can Never Belong” which perfectly show us how sad a sad scene can sound. I have to give Silvestri his flowers for giving all of this wonder. But if you want a happy, cheerful track, Bob’s your uncle! And by Bob, I mean “Stitch to the Rescue” which feels so heroic, as Stitch finally becomes a good guy. The film’s score had to push itself to the limits here, and I have gratitude that it did. And for the final category, you have the Elvis Presley songs, of “Stuck on You”, “Burning Love (Covered by Wynonna Judd)”, “Suspicious Minds”, “Heartbreak Hotel”, “(You're The) Devil in Disguise”, “Hound Dog” and “Can't Help Falling In Love covered in the picture by A\*Teens). These songs give this movie’s music a clear vibe it might not have had. **VERDICT: 20/20**

**In conclusion**, Lilo And Stitch (2002)
