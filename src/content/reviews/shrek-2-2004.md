###### **Shrek 2 (2004)**. What was once seen as a parody, became a sensation, and that movie was Shrek (2001), the considered classic of the 2000 decade. I recently saw the film in cinemas last month, as of writing this review, (Oops, I just broke the fourth wall) I consider this film a classic to this day. Shrek was so acclaimed, and well at the box office, that three years later, it even got its very first sequel! This film is considered the sequel at the time of DreamWorks Animation Studios, that would forever be the greatest of all time. But is this film a true all-star masterpiece, or should the original have stayed a standalone, all this while? SPOILERS!

###### Story

###### As Shrek and DreamWorks’ first ever sequel, Shrek 2 had a tough challenge DreamWorks had not yet faced at the time: expanding a movie franchise. And they did a superb job, because the Shrek franchise is all about the romance of Shrek and Princess Fiona. What I love about this picture is that it perfectly expands the romance story. The story is powerful, when it focuses on the heart, because it is very prominent and makes a great message: it does not matter on what you look outside, but what is inside of you. The references are all over the place, and make so much sense, because fit in the movie’s scenes. They are way more than what meets the eye, and what meets the eye is just generic Disney tropes. The climax works in the film’s favor, as it is the perfect way to conclude Shrek and Fiona’s character arcs. But, my favorite part of the story is the comedy, as the jokes are no longer laid back, but now all over the place. They are very funny, because of the lack of logic in the best possible way. For example: at the end of the dinner scene, when everyone says each other’s names, Donkey screams his name, and the main course just falls down. VERDICT: 20/20

###### Animation

###### The film looks exactly just as good as the original. First off, the criticisms: the film knows that the love of the game is to make the character designs look as real as possible, and I have seen better. I mean, just thinking about it gives me the heebie-jeebies, as a 3D, realistic-looking fairy tale can come off as weird and an unusual choice. However, this does not even remotely warrant the film potentially looking as ugly as everyone from the first movie sees the titular ogre is. In fact, this movie was considered to be top of the line at the time of its release. Fans really focused on the animation, and how the animators really put in the full maximum. In fact, my greatest praise is that Far, Far Away still looks like a kingdom’s of magic come to life. Of course it is supposed to be big, but the backgrounds support this, even outside the kingdom, such as Fairy Godmother’s factory or the Poison Apple Bar. I also love how with the letters near the kingdom, the kingdom looks like Los Angeles, California, but everything else looks like a medieval kingdom, perfectly cementing its tone, and I love the bursting color pallet attention to detail, as this fantasy should have! The characters, even with better designs still have incredible animation that perfectly fits the character of these people. However, if there is one thing I love most about the film, it is that the action scenes go hard, especially in the absolute powerhouse of a climax! Even by the standards of today, this film is beautiful! VERDICT: 18/20

###### Characters

###### Every original character in the film still has their charm, that they had all of those years ago. In fact, I would even say, that they have their best moments, yet! Shrek and Fiona have such incredible chemistry and moments together, but let us focus on them on their own. Shrek, now Fiona’s husband, gets invited to come to the kingdom by his mother-in-law and father-in-law. His fear of being rejected by his parents-in-law is because he is an ogre, not only cementing the message of the story, but giving him a flaw: his arrogance. But developing due to the love for his wife helps him learn from his flaws, and become a better person. This is a character arc at its finest. Donkey has a character arc as well of learning that it is okay for Shrek to find out by himself that he is a good friend to him. Seriously, he drinks a bottle he just saw that could be poisonous, just to ensure that Shrek lives on. Princess Fiona… is a bit of a disappointment, really. You can love her all you want, but I do not here. She is merely a POV character, which is not okay, when this is supposed to be her story. Yes, she does not know if she wants to make proud her parents, or make happy her husband, but I wish she did more in the story. The returning fairy tale mascots of the Gingerbread Man, Gepetto, Pinnochio, the pigs, wolf and blind mice all make the world feel lived in, once again, but this time, the side characters in the climax have more purpose to the story. However, my favorite characters are definitely the new ones. First off, Fiona’s mother Lillian, and especially her father Harold do not approve of her husband being an ogre, due to the princess having a destiny already: to marry Prince Charming, and I love their development and acceptance of their son-in-law, by the end. I also love certain clues of who he really is, which by the end, I love the execution of how he is actually a frog. Then, you have the antagonists, of Fairy Godmother and her son Prince Charming. First off, I love the slow burn execution of how Prince Charming has a character question of should he live his own life, or make proud his mother? Speaking of which, she is campy, flamboyant and threatening, all at the same time, and I love the subversion. But for my favorite character, I obviously pray for mercy from Puss In Boots, who learns to value Shrek as a good person, and even go from a villain-for-hire, to a hero. VERDICT: 19/20

###### Acting

###### Mike Myers is Shrek. Myers really has his charm and humor, but he does an even better than before. Eddie Murphy is known for his absolute hilariousness, and for Donkey, that is no exception, especially with the Bob Parker treatment joke, which is way too inappropriate for a PG movie, for the love of god! But Murphy still sells it so well! Cameron Diaz is Fiona. While I disliked Fiona as a character, Diaz really sells the naivety of the Princess, questioning her destiny. You really feel like the Ogre Princess is confused on if she should follow her destiny, or follow her dreams, and that is largely in thanks due Diaz’s portrayal. The fairy tale once again creatures are given the undying charm of Conrad Vernon, Chris Miller, Cody Cameron, Christopher Knights and Aron Warner. They all still have such different personalities, making this perfect world feel lived in. Julie Andrew’s and John Cleese have such great chemistry with each other and Diaz and Myers, as well as the emotion department. Jennifer Saunders and Rupert Everett as the villains are rich in chemistry, emotion and fear factor. Antonio Banderas is basically the casting choice the world needed as Puss, as his voice fits him like a glove.

###### Music&#xA;The main score is absolutely incredible. It really feels like it was made in 2004, even if it has indeed stood the test of time, all while having that classic fairy tale charm in every scene. However, it is the soundtrack that really makes the score feel different and like something else, in terms of quality. "Changes" is the most relevant to the story, as it represents the differences between Shrek’s life then vs. now. "Funkytown" and "I Need Some Sleep" represent how this is still a comedy, and the movie takes full advantage of that, by putting them in during the perfect scenes. Finally, "Accidentally in Love" and  "As Lovers Go" showcases how this is a romance. Now, normally I do not like licensed music in movies, but this is an exception. It works for the film’s pop culture references purpose and hits the scenes perfectly! But when the actors must lend their voices is when the songs are "Holding Out for a Hero" and "Fairy Godmother Song" by Saunders and “Livin' la Vida Loca" by Murphy and Banderas, as the actors really lend in their voices, and express their gifts of singing! VERDICT: 20/20

###### To conclude, Shrek 2 is a superior example on how a sequel can surpass a classic original and be even better than a movie that was great in the first place! Even with a few shortcoming, this film proves to be a timeless masterpiece of the art of fantasy, and a movie that I deem as beyond fantastic. It would therefore come to no surprise that I would recommend this to any Shrek fans, even to those who are not to be! Final Score: 97%
