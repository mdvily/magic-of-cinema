Open Season. As the earliest animated film of maybe the least acclaimed animation studio, fans and critics alike see this as a fun, yet uninspired flick of a bear turning wild. 20 years later, people are calling it generic, but is it? Or is it a fun flick about fighting back? SPOILERS!

Story

The story here is very generic. It is basically Madagascar (2005), where an animal is having fun at home, but they end up in the wild, where they learn to adapt to the wilderness. Yes, the film pus in a message of do not hunt animals, however what does the film really do with it, to set it apart? Nothing! Yes, the film is more about the charm than plot, but the charm is just fine. The heart is in the right place, but the comedy could be less mean-spirited, VERDICT: 9/20

Animation

A 3D animated movie, I get the feeling that this film cared about being a fun-looking film. And you know what? It succeeded! Remember that feeling where the character animations are good and designs are bad? To me, it is the opposite of what happened. The character animation looks like the film wants to be slapsticky, and nothing else. However, the character design looks like it has a lot of creativity. The color could have had more of its main color theme brown, but the action scenes are engaging, in their own ways. VERDICT: 16/20

Boog is our Beary (See what I did there?) annoyed protagonist, who just wants to live his life, and yet he realizes that the wildlife is his life. I like his character arc enough. It works, but it could be more different from the rest. Elliot, on the other hand is incredibly insufferable. He ruins Boog’s life for no reason and has no character arc, whatsoever. However, the other characters range from good to great. Beth is reasonably concerned for her bear son-figure. The side characters are annoying, yet iconic. You really feel like these characters have different personalities, making the world feel lived in. However, Shaw is a menacing, yet campy and humorous villain, and Boog in his house gave me the chills! VERDICT: 13/20

Acting

Martin Lawrence as Boog is arrogant, yet has a heart. Lawrence really gets his character, I just wish he did more to set himself apart. Ashton Kutcher, on the other hand, is insufferable as Elliot, begging you to laugh at his annoying deliveries. Debra Messing is Boog’s owner, Ranger Beth. She really has a heart for him, while selling it with her concern for Boog. Cody Cameron as Mr. Weenie is humorous, and his voice has a real… vibe. I love it. Jane Krakowski and Patrick Warburton are humorous enough as Elliot’s deer herd members Giselle and Ian. Billy Connelly, Demi Moore Nika Futterman, Jon Faverau, etc. are incredibly unique as the forest friends. However, Gary Sinise as Shaw is both humorous and menacing at the same time. To conclude, the acting is sometimes good, sometimes fine. VERDICT: 14/20

Music

Ramin Djawadi and Paul Westerberg were the ones behind the score and I think they did a fantastic job! It really sounds like some campy, fun flick in the woods. Exactly what the film is supposed to sound like. The action scenes never sound like they go hard. I wish they did, but that kind of is part of the film’s charm. But whenever there is a joke, they go all out. VERDICT: 19/20

To conclude, Open Season (2006) is a good enough charming, yet generic movie. I did not fall in love with the film, however, if you want a charming film, then this is your cup of tea! Final Score: 71%
