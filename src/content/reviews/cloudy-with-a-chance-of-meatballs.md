# Cloudy With A Chance Of Meatballs. Sony Pictures Animation did not have the greatest start of all time, with Open Season. But, Surf’s Up got them up the right pipe. Now, in 2009, SPA wanted to make something that was different from Open Season’s franchise, or a Penguin mockumentary, so they went off of the pages of Judi and Ron Barrett’s book in 1978. For a late 30 year anniversary, the studio chose to make a film about it. Surprisingly, this would be their most acclaimed film of the 2000 decade. I personally love the book, and find it very creative. But how good is this film? SPOILERS!

# Story

# I personally think that yes, the story takes creative liberties from the Barrett’s book, but that works in the favor of the film. It makes it less boring of a family film, due to character development. The story is more creative than what those might expect. It is a film about a young scientist, with aspirations, to turn a boring land into food paradise. However, it goes wrong. People get diabetes, and a paradise goes away, into a disaster. Right off the bat, Flint should not have been so careless. Maybe, the mayor should have hacked into Dr. Lockwood’s system, and Flint should learn to be careful who he trusts. The story is unpredictable, sure, but its decisions can be pretty stupid at times. However, this film is meant not to be analyzed, but for us to just have fun with it. It is merely like eating a nice marshmallow, before a few laughs. The film is very creative, with what it chooses to do with these foods, and overall, is just a hilarious movie, with plenty of laughs. However, its heart is prominent for both the story, and character development. VERDICT: 16/20

# Animation

# As I said, this is a movie about food. So the animation must do prominent things with its meals, snacks and treats. And sure enough, it does! From putting Flint and Sam in a jello bouncy house, to using Ice Cream as snow, the animation’s biggest highlight is its creativity. Now, this does detract from the character designs a tiny bit, but it still is a beautiful looking experience. Foods have a variety of color and so does this, and I love how its main color theme stands to red. Lastly, the action for both the spaghetti and meatballs apocalypse, and the final battle against FLDSMDFR are pretty intense scenes. VERDICT: 19/20

# Characters

Now, this is a cast of lovable characters, people! Our protagonist, Dr. Flint Lockwood, is a naive, yet genius young man, who wants to make a difference. Kudos to him, for having an incredible character arc, but his mistake would have been better executed, if he were better. How come Mayor Shelbourne did not learn software, hack into Flint’s project, and cause something dangerous to get him in trouble. It would make both of them better written. Speaking of which, Shelbourne is a bad villain. He accomplishes nothing by being nice, not fitting in with his character, and in the end, when his true nature is revealed, I feel like they did nothing with him. However, Reporter Samantha Sparks is girl power, at its finest. She has an arc about accepting herself, yet she is very capable of fending for herself. Manny is just there to be funny, and he succeeds. Baby Brett goes from being a jerk, to being a true team player. I love the development. Steve is the funniest monkey in a movie, since Abu from Aladdin (1992). However, my favorite character is Mr. Timothy Lockwood, Flint’s father, and the new patriarch, since Flint’s mother’s passing. He has an arc of not being shy, around his boy, and has robust reasons for not wanting his son to make these dangerous inventions, and he turns out to be right. Is he not iconic, in the logic department? VERDICT: 16/20
Acting
======

# Bill Hader as Flint Lockwood is smart, quirky, yet arrogant. Anna Faris as Sam Sparks is all over the place, in the best possible way. Andy Samberg as Baby Brett goes from funny, to fearless, just like the iconic chicken-man’s arc. Bruce Campbell as Shelbourne is snarky, and thinking he knows everything. Neil Patrick Harris and Benjamin Bratt as Steve and Manny do not do much for their roles, but nor do the characters with the dialogue. Therefore, it fits right in. Lastly, I love James Caan’s performance as Mr. Lockwood. It feels so prestige and powerful. That does not align the best with his character, but it works even better. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# The music can kind of take breaks of performing, at not the best times, however, it feels really well done, no matter what. It sounds so whimsical. Like Mary Poppins, but even more appropriate for the scene. However, there is the perilous side. It feels so dangerous, due to not only how it looks, but how it sounds. VERDICT: 17/20

# This movie is everything you could ask for if you want to have fun with a movie. The writing, acting, animation and score works well here. But even a critic can have a good time with this goofy adventure. I personally think out of all 5 of Sony’s original movies, that this is there most flavorous and delicious out of them. Well done, Chris Miller and Phil Lord! Final Score: 88%
