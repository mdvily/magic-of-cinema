# **Chicken Little (2005)**. Not only did Disney have a troubling production story upon the film, but they already made a different movie, 62 years before the film. The original script featured a female lead who goes to summer camp to overcome her anxiety, just to uncover a plot where wolves in sheep's clothing are trying to fatten up the campers for a feast. The single definition of a stupid movie premise. CEO Michael Eisner was furious. He demanded to switch the protagonist to a male trying to prove himself to his father. This was supposed to make it relatable. Then in order to blend something well with the “Sky Is Falling” premise, the crew was to add in aliens. Then, Holly Hunter, the lead actress left the movie, in order to give the role to Zach Braff, much more appropriate for the character. Honestly, what even is this absolute disaster? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# If there is one thing I can call the story, it is mean-spirited. Seriously! I feel bullied the entire time when I watch this movie, because there are no scenes that got even a smile out of me. I found the story so incredibly cruel, because there is an unnecessary amount of bullying in the movie. Seriously, due to Chicken Little’s mistake, I would be okay if a couple people bullied him. But he gets bullied by near his entire school! That is not okay, and it makes for some very unfunny jokes. Crazy Little Chicken? Ugly Duckling? That is very uninspired, and incredibly mean for no reason. On top of that, the heart is nonexistent. Everyone is incredibly mean to each other, so I never even shed a tear. I could not just think that the characters are any more than a bunch of jerks, so whenever they actually show kindness to each other, I do not care. But my biggest criticism is its structure and logic. First off, how would winning a baseball game get him on the good side of Oakey Oaks, or his father? Then, after said victory, an alien invasion happens. This is very out of place for this story, and the film’s rough pacing does both unconnected stories very poorly! **VERDICT: 0/20**

# **Animation**

# I fail to believe I am saying this, but the visuals are the greatest aspect of the movie. Because there are actually good things about it, or just two. One: the character animation is very fluid. The characters move very fluidly and accurately to how a human would move. They represent humans, so it works very well here. Two: A CG animated movie at the time period was a pretty daring risk. But all of my praise stops there. First off, the character designs look very ugly. They look like freaks, and the clothing they wear never affects their character. The action scenes are also terrible. If all you can get is characters running away from aliens, then the action scenes do the bare minimum. But my least favorite aspect is the color. Everything here is an ugly shade of dull orange and brown. Yes, this same thing happened with The Black Cauldron (1985) had the same problem, but it was pleasant to look at. Not this! **VERDICT: 5/20**

# **Characters**

# Honestly, the characters are my least favorite aspect of the movie, which says a lot. I hate this mean spirited cast of jerks so much, that there is nothing I can praise about the movie’s cast. Chicken Little is our loserous protagonist. And I call him a “loser” because he kind of is. The film never gives us a reason to like him in the first place. Said reasons would be, a relatable motivation, morality attributes, and understandable flaws. All the film does is make him think the sky is falling, and call it a day. One: why did the town believe him, and two: in real life, that does not make it an excuse to bully him. They fumbled the bag, so hard that I struggle to even take him seriously as or know why he is the main character. His three friends also suck. Abby Mallard is made to be his love interest, because there is no obvious reason not to. YES, THERE I- but anyway, you have his other friends, Runt and Fish Out Of Water. Seriously, what do they ever do other than make pop culture references? Literally nothing! Kirby is nothing but a mere plot device. Legitimately? I have no idea what character arc or traits he even has. Foxy Loxy is in my opinion, the villain, because she is the meanest to Chicken Little, which is important to the conflict. All she does is bully him, with no real reason why. But then there is Buck Cluck, Chicken Little’s father. My most hated character the canon has ever made. It is obvious that he should support his own son, but sides with the Oakey Oaks citizens in order to bully him. Yes, he acts nicer in the end, but one scene that entirely changes him, does not make for a compelling character arc. **VERDICT: 0/20**

# **Acting**

# Zach Braff is Chicken Little. Honestly, he was such a miscast, and I heard him foaming his voice in, the entire time. First, he sounds anything but a child. Second, he sounds so arrogant, but his role is anything but arrogant. So both the casting agency and Zach Braff had no idea what they were doing. Abby Mallard’s actress is Joan Cusack. Now, Cusack is a very talented actress, but I think that she has more of a pathetic voice, than her character allows. Kirby’s actors are Sean Elmore, Matthew Josten, and Evan Dunn. I guess they sound adorable, but why the heck would they hire three actors for one character in one age? Garry Marshall is Buck Cluck. Marshall has a voice way too kind for his character, and such loving chemistry to his son. But why? That is not accurate to his character one bit. Runt of the Litter and Fish Out of Water are portrayed by Steve Zahn and Dan Molina. And oh, my god. Their chemistry with each other along with the miscasting of Braff and Cusack is unacceptable. They did not even once come off as endearing to me. Foxy Loxy is portrayed by Amy Sedaris, with zero charm, only an annoying voice. Honestly, the acting here is nothing short of disastrous. **VERDICT: 1/20**

# **Music**

# Honestly, there is nothing good to say, other than one thing I somewhat liked. The theme with the aliens is kind of good. Nothing else works. There is no real main theme, just stupid, dated tunes. It relies so much on karaoke hits, and not actual music, that it becomes the film’s only identity. It also never helps that the songs fit very poorly into the picture, and are pretty annoyingly dated in the first place. None of the songs would fit if it were released today, and are just characters singing them, because they have nothing to do today. How abysmal, I must say! **VERDICT: 2/20**

# **In conclusion**, I hate Chicken Little so much. This movie is in my opinion one of the ugliest and most cruel movies I have ever seen, so I would recommend it to nobody. If you like this movie, I respect that, but I hate it so much. **Final Score: 8%**
