**Antz (1998)**. There is one reason on why people would remember DreamWorks’ first ever feature film: To compete with Pixar’s A Bug’s Life, during that exact same year. You see, Jeffrey Katzenberg had always wanted to become president of Disney, with Michael Eisner rejecting his offer, thus resulting in Katzenberg to create his own studio: DreamWorks Animation, and how this movie launched that rocket into outer space. But did DreamWorks have a happy beginning, or does this film deserve to be squished, exactly like how Mandible wanted the worker ants? SPOILERS!

Story

It is hard to quite predict what the story really is about? Is it about worker ant Z, who deems his life unacceptable, so he chooses to become a soldier, or does he run away with Princess Bala to Insectopia? This makes the narrative confusing, as you never know what the story even is about, making its premise far too confusing for my taste! Due to the plot lines being so different to each other, you feel like the story does not even know what it, itself is about! However, what ties them slightly together is the main theme of individualism, and being yourself, doing what you want to do in life. However, my favorite aspect is that the pacing takes its time to show you what the ant colony culture is like, even outside of the ant colony! However, the film takes too much time of telling you the message, and not showing it, which would very much benefit what the story could have been. Lastly, the comedy and themes are far too inappropriate for a movie that the MPAA has officially rated PG, even having the word, “erotic” in the script! VERDICT: 8/20

Animation

As the second ever animated feature, this is a film that can seem… dated at times, in a way that Toy Story was able to avoid, back in 1995, as the film looks like it was made at 3D’s infancy period, like how the rendering is weak, and the textures do look weird at times, along with the character animation looking… anything but smooth. However, this was made in the 90’s, where it was a state of the art! You even see how even if the character animation looks less than great, you still feel like the animators tried their best with it! To the point where some aspects, even over 37 years later, as of right now, still look gorgeous, such as Insectopia’s grand scale, and an ant’s point of view, in terms of the colony. The shots also do their jobs quite well, I just wish that the character designs were better, along with the color aspect. Also, history lesson: this was the first ever film to present digital water, which has actually aged quite well! VERDICT: 13/20

Characters

If I can name one thing I remember about Antz, even over a decade after watching it, it is the characters, but not in the best way. The problem is that the characters feel more like their actors than their selves! You have Z, our protagonist, who aspires to know, should he become a soldier, and later a war hero, or just a free ant of his own? This trope has been done so much better! You have his strong best friend Cutter, who supports Z, but questions his decisions, and his love interest, Princess Bala, who both loves Z, and gets tired of his dumb shenanigans. Barbatus is crucial to the story, for imprinting the idea in Z’s head, yet somewhat of a plot device. Lastly, the antagonists are the power hungry Mandible, who is a scary villain, despite being an ant, yet his plan is pretty stupid. Why would he want to kill every worker, if they are the backbone to the colony? I found my favorite and most complex character his assistant Cutter, who wants to be obedient and respectful to Mandible, yet has nowhere near the level of malice, as him. VERDICT: 12/20
