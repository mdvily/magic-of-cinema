Penguins Of Madagascar. I must say while I am not a die hard Madagascar fan, I have enjoyed the trilogy. The first was really good, the second one was humorous, and charming and the third one was just good. As for the TV show, I never saw an episode, but have heard good things about it. So, now that the TV shows go to King Julien, will Skipper, Rico, Kowalski and Private beat the odds? SPOILERS!

Story

When you hear the main plot, you get confused, very easily. Mostly, due to the question of… is there even a story? There is no real plot, only a bunch of characters just goofing around, until Private gets kidnapped. Then, the plot starts getting forced upon us. They could have just made this movie a TV special, but no, they made it a movie. In fact one of the movie’s biggest detriments is that whenever there is a good element, it forces it upon us. Especially the jokes, which are just stupid puns, and nothing else. Sometimes, they are actually funny, other times, they just fall flat, for obviously stupid reasons. Now, there are actually good elements, such as the pacing that actually makes sense for this kind of movie, and the message of realizing your self worth. These affects actually affect the story pretty well, and I must also factor in the attention to detail with the flowing of the scenes. VERDICT: 9/20

Visuals

Right off the bat, the character animation looks pretty bad, as the characters look like they just glitch into the next phase. That is not what character animation is supposed to do. At least the character designs are remarkable. They look like… I have no idea, but I know the student gets an A. The colors look mostly blue, perfectly reflecting the penguins and their antics. They start off white and they turn off blue. Either it be in the North Wind headquarters, or Dave’s lair. The technology also uses the squash and stretch technology. This works to affect the bonkers tone, even if it can get a little weird at times. But my favorite part here is the action scenes, as they go all out. From the final battle, to saving the egg from the leopard seals. The action scenes look so intense, due to them going all out, perfectly affecting the movie. Also, the backgrounds for every scene looks very well done. VERDICT: 16/20

Characters

I should probably mention the Penguins, themselves, to point out why this movie falls flat. The penguins are supposed to be the comic relief of the trilogy, a SIDE CHARACTERS, making them feel the same way in their own movie. But now they are in the movie of their own, the studio barely did ANYTHING to flesh them out. Skipper is the cynical smartmouth, Rico is the idiot who eats things out of the blue, Kowalski is the actually smart one, and Private is the sweetheart, who questions his position. The problem is that DreamWorks barely does anything to flesh these penguins out, other than that they think differently from the others, which almost never gets explored. The North Wind agents of the film are the harp seal Short Fuse, the owl Eva, who Kowalski has a crush on, Corporal, who loves Private, and would do anything to save him, and the wolf leader, whose name is not Classified, the penguins are just idiots, sometimes. They actually have distinct personalities, making the world feel lived in. Then, Dave is the antagonist, and he actually has a backstory you understand, although he could have been more of a threat. To conclude, the characters are tolerable, but not iconic. VERDICT: 10/20

Music

Need I say more? The music is fine, but they did next to the bare minimum with it. There is a panic mode, where the four run away from the Octopi, or save Private’s egg from the leopard seal. There is another version of it for a main theme, and while they should have had more originality, is it really that lazy? Finally, also there is a sneak theme, where the penguins sneak around.
