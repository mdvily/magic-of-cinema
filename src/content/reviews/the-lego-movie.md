# The Lego Movie. In 1932, the LEGO company was finally in session, so for its 82nd-year-anniversary,  Warner Brothers Animation recruited Phillip Lord and Christopher Miller to direct this acclaimed film, due to it creativity, animation, acting, song, comedy and heart. But is this film that good? SPOILERS!

# Story

# In a world where creativity is dying, The Lego Movie is a rare bird, due to it thriving in it. The film wraps itself around the LEGO toys, something never done before. It stars Emmet Brickowski, a normal person, tasked to put the piece of resistance on a weapon, referred to as the kragle. Yes, the film is a point one to point to movie, however, the creativity makes it less predictable and more fun. How so? You never know where these characters are going to go, due to the vastness of LEGO sets in the movie. It also helps that the picture has a lot of humorous comedy, but the heart, is even stronger. My favorite part though? The message on how anyone can be awesome. VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# This is not the first animated film to be stop motion, yet it is to be entirely out of LEGO toys. It perfectly fits the vibe of the movie, all while being unique. LEGOs are known for being colorful, well-textured and have amazing backgrounds. The movie takes advantage of this, perfectly. While being action-packed, the animated creativity is off the dang charts. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Emmet Brickowski is our main protagonist. He is incredibly lovable, and his passion to save the world, while having a fear that he is not awesome, makes him feel so real. His crush, Wildstyle/Lucy is essential to making the action stand out, all while actually having an arc of being less jealous of Emmet. Vitruvius, Emmet’s mentor is wise, and crucial, both to the story and the prophecy. Their teammates of Unikitty, Metal Beard, Benny and BatMan, along with the other master builders do an iconic job at making the world feel lived in. The villains President Lord Business and Good/Bad Cop are incredible villains, due to their designs, fear factor, and comedy, along with actual character arcs. VERDICT: 20/20

# Acting

# Chris Pratt as Emmet is charming, and conveys emotions perfectly. Elizabeth Banks really has that “cool” vibe as Wildstile, but sells it at the softer scenes. Allison Bree as Unikitty is adorable, Charlie Day as Benny is hyper in the best possible way, Nick Offerman as Metal Beard sounds like a real pirate and William Arnett as BatMan has that perfect voice for him. Liam Neeson and William Ferrell have both the comedy and the fear factor of both villains. However, Morgan Freeman as Vitruvius sounds like a vision. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# What I love about the music is that it has a vibe, you know? It sticks to tech-no music, to match the vibe of the film. I have no idea why it works, but it does. It knows when to be peaceful, energetic and sinister in all of the correct moments. However, the song “Everything Is Awesome” is iconic, due to its message, and iconic lyrics. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, The Lego Movie is a movie I was not initially hyped for. That means that I was pleasantly impressed. I consider this film to be one of my favorite ever animated films, that anyone should check out, in order to have an awesome time, and I do mean anyone. Final Score: 100%
