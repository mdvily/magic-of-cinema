# **Finding Nemo (2003)**. What if I told you that this was the darkest film in all of Pixar, with its opening alone? The film, directed by Andrew Stanton, the film was intended to be way darker, but the movie laid it back to be more appropriate. Still, the feature is considered one of animation’s best, with IMDb giving it an 8.2, Rotten Tomatoes gave it 99%, and MetaCritic helped it receive a 90/100. But is this really that good of a film, or do I wish that this was eaten by the barracuda? SPOILERS!

# Story

# Marlin (Albert Brooks) a widowed father with one son of many left, is an overprotective clownfish. When his rebellious son, Nemo (Alexander Gould) defies him to ruffle his gills, a boat accidentally kidnaps him. Now, Marlin and his Blue Tang friend Dory (Ellen DeGeneres) must embark on an epic journey, to find and rescue Nemo. Now, this story could easily be a lighthearted flick, but the story does not let that happen. How so? First off, it has the darkest opening out of any Pixar film. The film starts off as Marlin and Coral referring to their excitement for being parents. Then a barracuda comes out of nowhere, and eats Coral and 399 out of 400 children, making a kill count of already 400. That is dark. Then, if Marlin does not reach the deadline to rescue his son, he goes to Darla, who is notorious to the fish for shaking the life out of them in bags. This means that Nemo could come close to death. However, the comedy is relatively laid back. The jokes come from character interactions and actors’ deliveries. The pacing had one job: to fit the scene lengths well, and I would say it deserves a promotion! Then there is the heart, which comes a lot from the interactions between Nemo and his father, Marlin. The film also utilizes its setting quite well! The ocean can be wonderful, yet an abyss, and depending on the scenes, it takes advantage of it. However, my favorite story aspect is the way it teaches parents to let their children live their own lives and children to respect their parents. As a boy who is excited to be a father, I have my worries of how I might make mistakes. Yet, this film teaches me I might do a great job! VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# As I said, the ocean can be wonderful, yet an abyss, and depending on the scenes, it takes advantage of it. This means that the textures must have Pixar’s charm and accuracy to actual ocean habitats. I checked the habitats and am pleased to inform you that this crewmate did the task exceptionally well. The character animation moves extremely accurate to their respective animal. This does not mean, however that they do not feel like they are in a movie, and I love it. The designs, speaking of which, never try to look too much like humans, unlike Shark Tale, the year later. It tries to look like fish in an animated film. I love how it does not fall into that trap. The action scenes, say the chases and the just keep swimming climax are intense. However, the color is the greatest aspect, as the film looks so full of the perfect amount of energy in every single scene. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Marlin is our protagonist. They could have made him an unlikable character, who worries for no reason. That, however, is not the case. Yes, Marlin is an overbearing father, yet we understand WHY: He worries about losing Nemo, as he already has lost his mother and 399 brothers and sisters. But he learns to give Nemo the independence he needs. Speaking of which, Nemo still has a problem himself: He consistently disrespects his father. However, he learns to respect Marlin and becomes more brave, so I love the clownfish character arcs. Dory, on the other hand, could have done more at having purpose to the story, or even a character arc, but here we are. Yet it is the side characters that are my favorite aspects of the movie are the side characters. You have Nigel, the bearer of good news (I love the subversion), who helps Marlin and Dory find Nemo, the Tank Gang of Bloat, Bubbles, Deb and Flo, Gurgle, Peach and Jacques, who help Nemo escape, the Sharks of Bruce, Anchor and Chum, who try to convince the Orange and Blue fishes that they do not want to eat them, (It does not go well) and Crush and his son Squirt, the vibing turtles who are mirrors to the clownfishes, as how they have such a charming and positive relationship. However, my favorite character is Gill, the leader of the Tang Gang, due to him perfectly going from seeing Nemo (He calls him Sharkbait) as a means to get what he wants, which is to escape, to treating Sharkbait like a friend of his. The side characters are just a tip of the iceberg of making this vast, large ocean feel so lived in, as it should. VERDICT: 19/20

# Acting

# Albert Brooks is Marlin. Brooks is known for being in this movie worried, yet eccentric. You really buy his chemistry between Alexander Gould and Ellen DeGeneres. Speaking of which, Alexander Gould is Nemo. Gould feels rebellious and worried for his life, just like Nemo, his character with quite the voice. This young man really cares about both the film and his character. Ellen DeGeneres is Dory, and Dory is all over the place. I usually do not like that from an actress or actor. However, DeGeneres cares about her role, and this fits her character so well. Brad Garrett, Allison Janney, Stephen Root, Austin Pendleton, Vicki Lewis and Joe Ranft are the Tank Gang and I love how they have such different personalities, making such a small world of a tank feel so lived in. The late Barry Humphries is Bruce. Humpheries has such an iconic Australian voice, that it makes this feel like more of Australian. Andrew Stanton as Crush is so charming and has such a vibe. He feels like a surfer from the place, and given his character, that is a massive compliment! However, my favorite portrayal is Williem Dafoe as Gill, as I was surprised how dark he was willing to make his portrayal. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# The music here by Thomas Newman delivers everything you would want from Pixar’s 5th ever movie. “Field Trip”, “The Turtle Lope”, “Friends Not Food” and “First Day” deliver on the excitement level. The intense scenes of “Swim Down”, “Gill”, “Barracuda” and “Swim Down” are the finest examples of the movie sounding intense. But if you want the title sound, look no further than “Wow”, “News Travels”, “The Little Clownfish From the Reef” and especially, “Finding Nemo: Nemo Egg (main title)”, which all sound iconic. Every theme has a purpose to the story, and never feels like it is there, because it can be. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, Finding Nemo is a borderline perfect movie. It is such a fun movie on the surface, yet a powerful film at its core, basically parenting 101. It would come as no surprise that I would basically recommend this to anyone who likes movies, animals, or heck, anything, really! Final Score: 99%
