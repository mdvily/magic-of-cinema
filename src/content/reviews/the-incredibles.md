# **The Incredibles (2004)**. Pixar’s 6th movie, and the first one to be about human characters, this is arguably the most acclaimed movie Pixar has ever made. Fans love its story, logic, action, heroes, villain and music so much that it has an 8.0 on IMDb, and 97% on rotten tomatoes. Seriously! This film is considered to be the best superhero movie of the 21st century, or potentially ever! But is this movie really that super of an animated feature? SPOILERS!

# Story

# The Incredibles is considered one of Pixar’s greatest movies, and the story is a key factor of that. First off, as Shrek subverts fairytale tropes, The Incredibles does it with superhero tropes. For example, name one other Superhero movie that sues our heroes. Not only because it feels different, but because supers getting sued would undeniably happen in real life. It helps that the film is willing to take itself seriously. The comedy and heart works well, due to it appealing to kids well, and adults exceptionally. I love how it tries to set itself apart from the previous 5 movies. It not only tries to be about humans, but to be a bit more realistic. The pacing is methodically slow-paced, perfectly making the incredible segments feel earned. The action is clever, in terms of both showing the successions and failures of the family. However, my favorite part is that it gets very dark at times. Mr. Incredible is having a midlife crisis, Elastigirl thinks her husband is cheating on her with another woman, and Syndrome’s plan is literal genocide! When did family movies stop getting so dark?! To conclude, I also love how the Parr family, other than the father Bob, does not want to be a family of superheroes. Mrs. Helen Parr wants to be a stay-at-home mother, Violet/ the daughter is ashamed that she even has powers, Dash has aspirations just to join the school’s sports team, and Jack-Jack is just an infant. PS, I love how relatable the family dynamics and theme of family, as well as how much creativity it has with its 1952 retro future. VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# The film looks both creative and nostalgic, perfectly getting right how the world would look if technology is a noticeable part of both Superhero lives and real life. The color pallet takes more shades than anything else of Red. It perfectly represents how incredible the action scenes (You will get to them later) can be, in my opinion. The character designs and animation looks so real and I love how it feels like it could arguably be a part of real life. I also love the attention to detail, for example, the more confident Violet gets, the more she puts her hair back. However, the action is is energetic, but that is not why I love it so much. I love it so much, because it perfectly demonstrates what these characters are capable of. It makes Dash fast, Violet’s annoyance at criminals, Bob’s strength, and Helen’s limb-stretching abilities. It really feels like a superhero movie, at its best-looking. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Mr. Incredible/Robert “Bob” Parr is our protagonist. He is incredibly relatable, as many grown men can relate to both wanting to live the good old days, and tire of their jobs. I also love how the film takes its time to establish his love and development of learning to love his wife and children. Elastigirl presents girl power perfectly, not letting her just be concerned, as she also gets annoyed at her husband for lying to her, to the point where when she cries over his lies, we cry too. Violet is ashamed for her superpowers, and Dash is a sweet, yet over energetic, making them feel like actual children. Jack-Jack as a baby, in terms of his powers perfectly represents how infants like him can be very unpredictable. Enda E Mode is their costume designer, with such a flamboyant and campy personality. Syndrome is Pixar’s best antagonist, in my opinion, as he can be funny, yet terrifying. However, Mr. Incredible rejecting him, turning him into the antagonist, makes him feel like an actual foil to our hero. However Lucius Best/Frozone is my favorite character. He feels very reluctant, yet somewhat excited to being a superhero again. VERDICT: 20/20

# Acting

# Craig T. Nelson as Mr. Incredible is exhausted from the start, yet classy and empathetic at the end. Holly Hunter as Elastigirl is both mad at her husband, yet concerned for him. Jason Lee is amazing at delivering lines, making him feel cartoonish in the best way, to the point where I think he’s better, than how good Jack Black could have been as him. Sarah Vowell feels like an actual teenager as Violet. Spencer Fox is energetic and a blast of fun as Dash. Brad Bird himself is Edna Mode, with a campy personality. You really get the gist of how flamboyant his character is. However, Samuel Jackson is Frozone is full of lines, and basically the Nick Fury of his time. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# The score by Michael Giacchino is classy and unconventional. That, and the sound effects and music was all recorded on analog tapes, making it really feel like it is from the 60’s. You really get the feeling that the movie’s main theme was inspired by the theme of James Bond, however it has its own superhero feel, making it actually work for a superhero film. It’s full of life during and heroic scenes, and creepy for the antagonist themes. You really get the feel that Giacchino put in every last ounce of effort for the music here, and all of it comes to good use, making it almost as good as Up (2009). VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, there is a reason that actor Jack Black said that this was one of his all time favorite films. The Incredibles (2004) is an absolute delight of a movie, that any avid superhero fan should definitely check out. It is a film that I simply cannot recommend enough, due to it being one word: Incredible. Final Score: 100%
