# Corpse Bride. This is potentially one of the most acclaimed animated films to dawn on Halloween and the dead. This does not make it an automatic masterpiece, but this is a very… different Halloween movie. But how? SPOILERS!!!

# Story

# Now, this Halloween movie is different. It does not talk about demons, or haunting. It talks about true love. And while yes, the message of love can be on the nose, at times, and the comedy, while humorous and flavorful, does not have a real purpose in the story, it is the heart that works. It takes its time to address what the characters want, and gives them all reasons to be nice or mean to each other. That, and the romance is never rushed, and it leads you to places you would not expect first time. VERDICT: 14/20 

# Animation

# The colors of both worlds perfectly affect the atmosphere. The land of the dead is supposed to be, sometimes sad, sometimes whimsical. The colors represent that, however, they are shades of grey in the land of the living, because of how strict everything is in terms of marriage and love. The action scene between Victor and Lord Bittern is enticing and visceral. The character animations might show their age eventually, but I do not think that they have yet. However, the character designs, in my opinion, are a mixed bag. Occasionally, they look good, but sometimes… Their designs looking finished is not always a good thing. VERDICT: 18/20

# Characters

# The characters on average in the script are… Good. Victor Van Dort is our clumsy protagonist. Yes, he develops well, however, he had no reason to lie to Emily. Instead, he could have told her the truth, and she would probably have helped him anyways. Speaking of which, Emily is the film’s best character. She has a powerful arc of being at peace, and that works here, because in the first place, she had a good reason to be angry. She married a mad she hated, who robbed and murdered her. Victoria Everglot is one-dimensional, yet sympathetic. Barkis Bittern is a villain to write home about, due to there being real people like him: greedy, power hungry and abusive. The maggot is Emily’s companion, and adorable. Yes, he can be discouraging, but he cares for her. Gutknecht is the worst main character in the movie, because he barely has a purpose to the plot. However, the parents of Victor and Emily are strict, and a force to be reckoned with, without having to abuse their kids. VERDICT: 14/20

# Acting

# Emily Watson as Victoria is beautiful, and desperate to not marry someone, she does not love. Enn Reitel as the Maggot is adorable, yet is so soft, it can make you cry. Michael Gough as Gutknecht is wise, and powerful when he is responsible, but not very humorous when he is not. Richard E. Grant as Lord Bittern is chilling, due to Grant knowing that his character has riches and power, so until the end, he can walk out of any trouble. However, the two actors who feel like and get their characters the best are Johnny Depp as Victor and Helena Bohnam Carter as Emily. VERDICT: 19/20

# Music

# We begin with According To Plan. It represents how Victor and Victoria’s parents are cruel, and caring about money, only. Yes, it sets the tone of the living world well, but they could have been showing it, instead of telling it. For Remains Of The Day, Bonejangles sings to Victor, Emily’s backstory, and how she married Lord Bittern, who killed her afterwards. But, why did Emily not tell him herself? Tears To Shed represents Emily’s pain, and how she wants a happy ending, to her insect friends. It’s beautiful, and it makes you feel for her. The Wedding Song represents Victor and Emily’s “marriage” in the land of the dead. It is very important to the story, and we get to see both Victor and Emily’s perspectives. But the highlight is the beauty of Victor playing piano, both with Emily and to Victoria. VERDICT: 14/20

# In conclusion, this Tim Burton movie is a borderline classic. It definitely has its flaws, but this is a very fun time. Final Score: 79%
