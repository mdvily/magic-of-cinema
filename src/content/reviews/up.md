# **Up**. As the end of Pixar’s golden age and 10th movie of the studio, this film is widely considered the some of the studio’s greatest work yet, which is a very high bar. 14 years after the studio came to be, people are saying that this film has stood the test of time. My mother loves it, my father loves it and my younger brother loves it. But do I? SPOILERS!

# Story

# Right off the bat, May I just say that the opening and third act are so amazing, I could never do it justice with just words! The plot for those times consists of charm and a heart that can actually make you cry! However, the point that I must emphasize is that the middle chunk of the movie could be better. It mostly just consists of comedy and adventure that has been done to death before. I hardly ever laughed, and the film has a trope of “Guy meets his hero, hero is actually villain” has been done to death before. But even in the worst part of the film, there are still great scenes to be found, if you know where to look and find it, for example, the he. Muntz kidnaps Kevin, setting the house on fire, or when we find out, along with Carl and Russell his kill count. To conclude, the story is both amazing and mediocre in different scenes, making it just… good. VERDICT: 14/20

# Animation

# The film has both a beautiful score and a gorgeous animation style. Out of all of the films Pixar has ever made to have a setting in the sky. It looks beautiful, with a lot of beautiful color pallet, leading going to Sky Blue (The color, not the backwards studio). It really gives the film a vibe. The textures and character designs look gorgeous. I do not feel like its simplicity drags down its execution. However, the action scene in the final battle is engaging, and easy to follow. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Carl Fredricksen as our protagonist is easy to feel for. He lost the love of his life, and in his opinion, his life! Does that not feel so sad? Russell as his son figure by the end of the movie is the comic relief. Sometimes, he brings a good amount of laughter. Other times, I wish he was not in the movie. Ellie is the driving purpose to the story. Her existence gives the plot its power, making her feel welcomed here. Our sidekicks Dug and Kevin are humorous make the worlds feel lived in. Lastly, Charles Muntz is our antagonist. Muntz is a threatening villain, however, his traits of selfishness and paranoia could be better explored. VERDICT: 17/20

# Acting

# Jordan Nagai as Russell is charming, yet he can get a bit annoying at times. Bob Peterson as Dug is incredibly charming. You really buy his stupidity, and kindness. Peterson also plays Alpha, and actually buys a threat. Delroy Lindo, Jerome Ranft and Josh Cooley are the rest of the dogs, and while they can be humorous, they sell the fear factor. The film’s director Pete Docter as Kevin has a lot of stupidity, and fear. However, two of the greatest portrayals of all time, in terms of animation are Christopher Plummer and Ed Asner. May they both rest in peace due to both of them making for an amazing cast. VERDICT: 18/20

# Score

# The score is made by Michael Giacchino, and he is an excellent score composer. However, what is his greatest musical score? Simple: Up (2009). Every beat is able to make you cry, with an iconic main theme. Seriously! I have no knowledge of a person who has watched this movie, and does not have a clue of the score. It has a beauty theme and a panic theme, that both affect the scene perfectly. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, so I find the film overrated? A bit. Yet, I still find a movie like this a borderline amazing film. I really appreciate this film so much. Final Score: 89%
