# **The Princess And The Frog (2009)**. Once upon a time, Disney studios wanted to make a movie about E.D. Baker’s novel The Frog Prince (1812) tale. The problem? E.D. Baker’s novel The Frog Princess was already an adaptation, and Disney knew just ripping the book in 2002 would be lazy. So, they had to make changes. Big changes. First off, Tiana (Based on actual waitress Leah Chase) was going to be named Maddie, which was considered, as Tiana is Disney’s first black princess, too derogatory. I cannot say the word, but I will say it would cost the movie and studio millions. So, they changed it to Tiana. Then, to be less racist, they turned Tiana from a maid to a waitress. Then, along with bringing 2D they turned the title into The Princess And The Frog. If you ask me, I would call the Disney Post Renaissance a good, and very underrated era, in terms of movie quality. But is this a good closure to the era, or should we just abandon it at the local swamp? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# The story here starts off very promising! The movie tries to tackle a waitress, a black protagonist, and how you need to work for your dreams to come true. The studio has never done that before! And for the first act, it actually works! You see, what I love most about the story is that it tries new things. It also helps that the message of hard work is inspiring, and there is plenty of heart to put in the picture. The characters interact together a lot, and the relationships become less toxic. This makes me shed a tear, because I find humanity in the film. Even the Disney cliches actually work, because they are put into the plot in a very clever way. However, there are quite some bad aspects. First off, there are plenty of cliches that are very rare chances to happen. Why did the lightning strike at the exact place the balloons Tiana and Naveen were holding on to? Why did Louis and Ray have to be at the exact place they were at, and not just study where they were? How did Tiana turn into a frog, after kissing Naveen? Then, you have the second act. It really disappoints me, because they took a frog fairy tale. Okay, did they do with it? Not much memorable, really. It was never bad, just a bit of a letdown. Also, the comedy kind of sucks. The film spends so much comedy on the gross out humor, which does not go together well with me. But if you can get past that, then this story is perfectly fine. **VERDICT: 12/20**

# **Animation**

# This film looks absolutely beautiful, In every sense of the word. I criticized the film a lot for the story, but the animation is a massive exeption, and an escape from my criticisms. First off, the colors are gorgeous! They take shades of green, a bit of blue and teal. They represent the beauty and Tiana’s depiction of the swamp, perfectly. The characters look like actual species, rather they be humans, or animals. The character designs were studied and studied and improved well. Then, there is the character animation. The film looks like it might have come out yesterday, and it looks beautiful. The character animation is very fluid, to the point where the characters move exactly like they would in real life. No action segments required! Then, you have the backgrounds. They took notes from both New Orleans of the 1920s, and how they used CinemaScope with Lady And The Tramp (1955( Next review!)) to make this look unique. Finally, Disney went back to 2D animation. This looks incredible, and nostalgic to me! **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Characters**

# The characters in The Princess And The Frog (2009) on average are… good. You have Tiana. As a protagonist, she works, but I wish that the film did more with more. Her motive is to get a diner of her own, and because she works hard, that her goal feels reasonable. Tiana also is a great role model to others who have a real motive in life. The problem? Once the second act starts, her character arc kind of turns to dust. Then you have Naveen. Yes, he learns to put in more hard work, but it just comes out of nowhere. For so much of the movie, others do the work for him. Say, for example, Tiana cuts up some food for him, instead of teaching him. You then have Louis and Ray, as the side characters of this fantasy. Louis was pretty funny. I loved his motive of wanting to be a jazz musician, because it gives him a real vibe. I just wish he had more of a character arc. Ray, on the other hand, is so iconic! He is absolutely hilarious, and incredibly kindhearted. But then, in the end, his death gets serious and unexpected. Mama Odie is basically the whimsical motherly fairy tale. Only, she has one scene. One. Iconic. Scene. It is a perfect way to establish the protagonists’ flaws, and how they can develop. Then, Dr. Facilier, the antagonist is great! He has a real vibe that most Disney villains do not have. I just wish that he had more time to actually do evil things, and not puppeteer around. Seriously, one of his worst deeds is stepping on a bug. **VERDICT: 14/20**

# **Acting**

# The Princess And The Frog has plenty of references to black people, so they had to hire some black actors and actresses. Anika Noni Rose for example, is Tiana. Noni Rose has a lot of charm and kindness into her character, but can have attitude when need be. Bruno Campos is Prince Naveen. Campos is way better at being a lawyer than he was in this movie. He depicts Naveen, in my opinion, like a lazy dictator, but that just is not who his character is. Michael-Leon Wooley is Louis the jazzy alligator. Wooley is so charasmatic, and easy to love. He has a real vibe, and kind demeanor I wish these Disney characters had. Jim Cummings Is Ray. Oh my god, Ray is such a likable character, but he sounds so annoying! Cunnings gives off a voice that just does not match Ray’s personality. Jenifer Lewis is Mama Odie. Lewis basically turns into a sassy godmother, and has one heck of a vibe, at that! Finally, Keith David is Dr. Facilier, and my personal favorite part of the entire movie. David sounds so campy, yet has the exact character that Facilier needs to be fantastic as a character, **VERDICT: 16/20**

# **Music**

# The main score for the movie is pretty good. Yes, the jazz in certain scenes can be a bit weird and nonsensical. But if you really hear the music, it sounds authentic, and can be more calm with the more peaceful scenes. But the songs are just fantastic! "Down in New Orleans (Prologue & Main)“ presents to us how New Orleans in the 1920s in their world. My personal favorite part is the attention to the detail, in terms of description. But if you want the I Wish song, then Bob is your uncle! Or should I say "Almost There" is? The only song that I do not like very much. Why? Because, while I do like the artstyle it uses for this occasion only, it builds up a real goal for Tiana. The problem? It just disappears from the movie, and I, frankly, do not know why. This makes the song useless. But, back to the good tracks! "Friends on the Other Side" by Facilier is the most catchy, and almost best at explaining a Disney villain’s motive. You really get a read on Facilier from the moment the song happens, but it makes him so mysterious, that you want to learn more. "When We're Human" is basically "Almost There", but builds up ACTUAL hype for what happens next in the story. Why? It actually happens. The catchy songs that feel so much like Disney are "Gonna Take You There" and "Dig a Little Deeper". But, I personally enjoy the second option, because it has more story importance, and necessity to even be here, period. Not to say that I dislike the first option! I personally liked "Never Knew I Needed", because it sounds like it was from this movie, which gives it a vibe. However, my personal favorite song from this movie is "Ma Belle Evangeline", because it utilizes the setting the best, which is actually a golden rule for cinema. **VERDICT: 18/20**

# **In conclusion**, The Princess And The Frog (2009) is a great and very charming Disney movie. Now, I wanted to like this movie like how a little girl who likes frogs would, I really did! But, the problem is that even though I enjoyed this movie pretty much, I tolerated the writing, but it still had its problems. Final Score: 80%
