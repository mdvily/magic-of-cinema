# **WALL.E (2008)**. Director Andrew Stanton is well known in the PIXAR industry, for directing the films of A Bug’s Life, Monsters, Inc, and Finding Nemo. However, who would have known his most acclaimed film of his PIXAR career is arguably about a boy and a girl robot, saving humanity, or even guessed? It has an 8.4 on IMDb, a Rotten Tomatoes score of 95%, and a MetaCritic user score of 8.9! Now, this does occasionally make films overrated, but is this the case, or does it deserve the praise it gets? SPOILERS!

# Story

# The story is just iconic in a way others cannot match. First off, the story has a first act that expresses so much power and such a powerful message on let the earth thrive, without uttering a single word. However, once they aboard the AXIOM, that is when, in my opinion, the movie truly becomes something special. It feels so human, because there are so many scenes, where not only do WALL.E and EVE express their love, but the film tests if they even have it in the first place. This makes the pacing on the slow side, but this makes the story feel logical and real. It also makes the heart in the right place. VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# This film looks beautiful, in a way other PIXAR movies cannot match, due to it having a different vibe. First off, the color pallet leans to shades Blue, Black and Purple, perfectly feeling like Outer Space. The textures of the AXIOM ship look like an actual ship made by robots. The action scenes of the robots fighting are so easy to follow, and while there is some shaky cam, that somewhat is the point, and it is dialed back, by a lot. However, my favorite aspect of the animation is the character design. They were free to make the robots look however they wanted, and it really shows. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Let us begin with our protagonist, WALL.E, who really wants to make do with the possessions he has in the first act. However, in the last two acts, they turn a robot into a character more likable than most animal or human heroes of the studio’s work. His love interest EVE, is basically the same thing, however, her having a love discovery for WALL.E gives her a real human appeal. M.O. is very annoying, however that is a very good thing here, due to that being the point. Lastly, Mary, John and McBrea have such personality, and character development, I just wish it did not from the start present every human as someone who did not anymore care about earth. Finally, making AUTO have no character is a mixed move. Yes, it makes him different, like a mirror, to our protagonists, but no personality makes him boring. VERDICT: 18/20

# Acting

# Ben Brutt is WALL.E, and he is able to convey such emotion without even saying many words! The exact same thing applies with Elissa Knight as EVE. Jeff Garlin is Captain McBrea, and he sells the arrogance of his character in the start, however, he kills the determination in the second act, making him sound… real. Brutt also portrays M.O., and while he is annoying in this role, that is a good thing here. John Ratzenberger and Kathy Najimy are John and Mary, with such amazing humanity and chemistry here, they feel like an actual real-life couple, for quite a long time. Finally, AUTO is portrayed by an AI referred too as MacInTalk, which was a great choice, due to the character having no personality in the movie, whatsoever. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# The music sounds in a way, magical! Every beat sounds so iconic, especially when WALL.E and EVE board the AXIOM, where the music goes from transcendent to perfect. The entirety of the film sounds so artificial, which is exactly the point, even in the action scenes, due to the film wrapping itself around AI. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, my father loves this movie so much, and I personally agree with him. WALL.E (2008) is a delightful movie, that by the end, will teach you to value Earth, and do more to keep it a safe and healthy planet. I would recommend it, however, I am sure you have already watched it, at least once. Final Score: 98%
