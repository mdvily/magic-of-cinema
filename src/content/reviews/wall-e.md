**WALL.E (2008)**. Director Andrew Stanton is well known in the PIXAR industry, for directing the films of A Bug’s Life, Monsters, Inc, and Finding Nemo. However, who would have known his most acclaimed film of his PIXAR career is arguably about a boy and a girl robot, saving humanity, or even guessed? It has an 8.4 on IMDb, a Rotten Tomatoes score of 95%, and a MetaCritic user score of 8.9! Now, this does occasionally make films overrated, but is this the case, or does it deserve the praise it gets? SPOILERS!

Animation

This film looks beautiful, in a way other PIXAR movies cannot match, due to it having a different vibe. First off, the color pallet leans to shades Blue, Black and Purple, perfectly feeling like Outer Space. The textures of the AXIOM ship look like an actual ship made with robots

Acting

Ben Brutt is WALL.E, and he is able to convey such emotion without even saying many words! The exact same thing applies with Elissa Knight as EVE. Jeff Garlin is Captain McBrea, and he sells the arrogance of his character in the start, however, he kills the determination in the second act, making him sound… real. Brutt also portrays M.O., and while he is annoying in this role, that is a good thing here. John Ratzenberger and Kathy Najimy are John and Mary, with such amazing humanity and chemistry here, they feel like an actual real-life couple, for quite a long time. Finally, AUTO is portrayed by an AI referred too as MacInTalk, which was a great choice, due to the character having no personality in the movie, whatsoever.

Music

The music sounds in a way, magical! Every beat sounds so iconic, especially when WALL.E and EVE board the AXIOM, where the music goes from transcendent to perfect. The entirety of the film sounds so artificial, which is exactly the point, even in the action scenes, due to the film wrapping itself around AI. VERDICT: 20/20
