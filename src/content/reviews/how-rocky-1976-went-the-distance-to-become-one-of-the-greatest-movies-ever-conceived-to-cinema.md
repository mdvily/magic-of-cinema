# **Rocky (1976)**. March 1975 was the month where journeyman boxer Chuck Wepner took champion Muhammad Ali, even knocking him down in 15 rounds. After Sylvester Stallone watched this in a theater, he was inspired to write a movie about a blue-collared fighter who would go the distance. Stallone only had $106 in his bank account. After he wrote the script, he shared it to producers Irwin Winkler and Robert Chartoff. The two offered Sylvester $360,000 on the condition that Robert Redford or Burt Reynolds would star as Rocky Balboa. Stallone refused, so he could play Balboa. In order to fill the 8,000-seat Olympic Auditorium for the climactic fight, the production advertised free chicken dinners to anyone who would want to act as a crowd extra. Then Mr. Stallone’s family joined in the movie. His father was the timekeeper, his brother was the street corner singer, and his then-wife was the photographer of the set. The film grossed $225 million, and is considered to be a classic of the 70’s, to this day. But, is it so? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# Rocky (1976) is about a man named Robert “Rocky” Balboa, or The Italian Stallion who is considered a loser by many, including his coach Mickey Goldmill. Balboa finally gets the chance to box against champion Apollo Creed. With the support of his girlfriend Adrian Pennino and her brother Paulie, can Balboa go the distance? The story is masterful, because of many reasons. First off, I never found anything dated. I just thought it was relatable, when Rocky flirted with Adrian. I relate to it, because, when my father asked out my mother, It was clumsy. I relate to Rocky’s antics, because I might do the same thing, in time. Now, on to the undeniable positives! This is not a predictable movie, or a generic one. The film is less about boxing, and more about Rocky as a character. That, and Rocky does not actually win in the end. He just goes the distance, and never gives up. That is enough to finally knock Creed down. That, and the pacing is very slow. It works because I started to believe Rocky’s journey. The film would consistently take its time to both establish the story, and Rocky’s character. The tone also is more realistic, and the dialogue supports it. But it still has comedy, and I laughed a lot. The jokes always feel in character with the characters. But my favorite aspect is the heart. Rocky is very kind to others, despite his mistakes, and Adrian and her brother Paulie are in return. The film knows how to make it both heartfelt and real. Rocky is not perfect, and film uses its pacing and story structure, in order to make it work. Rocky’s relationship with Adrian is not perfect. It is heartfelt, but it has plenty of awkward moments. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Visuals**

# This film looks masterful. I honestly feel like the visuals are noticeably underrated, because people focus more on the writing, music and acting. First off, the film has such a color aesthetic. Many films like Gone With The Wind (1939) are more colorful, and joyous. Rocky is a different case, though. The film uses its main color of black, for Rocky’s robe, and Apollo’s outfit. The black perfectly showcases to us, that it is not an action packed fantasy, but rather a slower paced tale. It still has red, white and blue in order to symbolize America. However, they are rather laid back. The movie also uses Steadicam, in order to look grand. Technology like that resulted in Rocky’s run up the Philedelphia Museum of Art to look grand. It still does today, and I cannot get enough of it. You also have Rocky’s look. In his next five movies, he would look more shredded. But here? He looks more soft and realistic. Like a fighter from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. I must as well factor in how the movie looks rather realistic. But my favorite aspect are the action scenes. They are so well choreographed, and look like they would actually happen. This is most true in the final matchup, when Rocky takes on Apollo. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Characters**

# The cast of characters here is some of the best and most underrated in all of cinema! We shall start off with the side characters, because I want to save my favorite for last. Adrian Pennino is Rocky’s girlfriend. The film perfectly addresses that women can be as strong as men. This is because when her brother calls her a loser, she snaps back, with logical points. This includes how she cleans for him, picks up his dirty clothes, cooks for him, etc. But she also has a soft side, where she supports her husband. One is where she kisses him good luck, before the final fight. Her brother Paulie is Rocky’s friend, who is more complex than he seems. The bad in him is that he yells at his sister, and treats her like garbage. However, he has a kindhearted relationship with Rocky. He even lets him practice punching with the meat in his store! This makes him more complex: should he stay mad at his sister, or listen to his friend? Mickey Goldmill is Rocky’s mentor. He starts off yelling at Rocky, and seeing him as a loser, saying "You're a bum, Rock. You're a bum!" But once he finds out a shot at redemption for both of them, he gives Rocky a chance to have him as his manager. He starts to become more believing to Rocky. I love how slow it is for him to see his potential, because it makes it feel earned. The film has no villain, but it does have a rival for Rocky. And that is Apollo Creed. Creed is a real challenge for Rocky, and his confidence perfectly matches out Rocky’s lack of self-esteem. I must as well factor in how he actually develops a respect for Rocky after round 15. Rocky Balboa is our main protagonist, and he is one of my favorite characters of all time. He starts off as a considered to be complete failure. But then, he up as an absolute champion. I love how he practices in many slow scenes, which makes the film feel earned. He was always kind and lovable, yet he never stops being relatable. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Acting**

# Talia Shire is Adrian Pennino. Shire has such an attitude towards Burt Young as Paulie. This is especially true, when she snaps at him, for calling her a loser. She goes hard, and Talia’s voice and body movements support it. But she also has a soft and kind side to Rocky. When she talks to him, her body movements are calm and more grounded and voice soothing. Burt Young is Paulie. Young is cruel and boisterous to his sister. Whenever he yells, I shiver, but I found a lot of humanity in the movie, whenever Young is more calm and cheerful. I love the difference between the two, because it affects Paulie as a character perfectly. Burgess Meredith is Mickey Goldmill. Meredith in the start sounds loud and verbally abusive. But across the movie, he ends up kinder to Rocky, and I love how he still has that loud voice, he just has more heart in there. Carl Weathers is Apollo Creed. Weathers does not sound callous and loud, but rather flamboyant and energetic. This actually is Apollo’s character, so I must praise the accuracy of it. His chemistry with Sylvester Stallone is also great. But speaking of which, Sylvester Stallone as Rocky Balboa is the quintessential portrayal of the movie. He makes his character sound so real, and puts in a tough, yet kind voice. His body movements are also incredibly accurate to Rocky’s character, depending on the scene. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Music**

# The music, by the legendary Bill Conti, is incredible. The soft scenes, in terms of music are "Philadelphia Morning" and “Alone In The Ring”, which make the film sound human. They demonstrate how the movie can sound more peaceful when need be. If the film were loud and action-packed the entire time, there would be no humanity. Fortunately, Conti does not fall into that trap. He knows perfectly how to make the film sound peaceful. "You Take My Heart Away," is a love ballad and the emotional anchor of the spine between Rocky and Adrian. I love how it sounds like it has such a 70’s aesthetic, yet pretty timeless. "Philadelphia Soul" is most of the training scenes, and I really think that it sounds like the movie is going hard. “Going The Distance” turns into “The Final Bell” in order to sound like two things. One is very accurate to a boxing match. Two is the perfect victory lap of how hard Rocky has trained. But then you have “Gonna Fly Now”. Oh my word, if this not one of the most iconic tracks. The choir, the instruments, the significance to the time period and Rocky’s character, everything. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **In conclusion**, Rocky (1976) is an incredible movie that you will be a changed man after watching. This movie slaps, in terms of the script, visuals, portrayals and music. It would therefore come as no surprise that I would recommend it to anyone. But the real reason on why the movie slaps so much is its legacy. This was not the first ever sports movie, but the first to win Oscar’s and be considered some of the best of the year. It paved the way for many other stories about working hard, and trying your best. This inspired many other movies such as The Karate Kid (1984), Rudy (1993), The Fighter (2010), Flashdance (1983) and School of Rock (2003). **Final Score: 100%**
