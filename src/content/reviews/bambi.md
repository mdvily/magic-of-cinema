# **Bambi (1942)**. Based on the Austrian Novella of Bambi: An Adventure In The Woods (1923), comes Disney’s 5th animated feature, about a deer learning the ways of life. This turned into an acclaimed feature with IMDb rating of 7.3 and rotten tomatoes and metacritic gave it a score of 91%. But is that well-earned? SPOILERS!

# Story

# The story is just a deer learning the ways of life, so how powerful could this film really be? The result? Let me refer to its slow pacing first, because in order to make the coming-of-age feel earned and natural, the pacing cannot be as fast as a deer. The pacing supports that incredibly well, making all of Bambi’s accomplishments feel earned. The film is also unpredictable. First watching it, I expected the film to fast forward to the end, where Bambi dies. But no, it gives him a happy ending. However, the tone is my favorite part of the movie. It feels very dark, in definitely 2 scenes. One, where the entire forest burns down, and two, when Bambi loses his mother. It works in the movie’s favor so much, due to the film not only feeling different, but never feels like it goes overboard, with the film having plenty of charm and humorous comedy. To conclude, the story, even 84 years later, still holds up perfectly! VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# Right off the bat, the colors look beautiful! They take shades of yellow, green and blue for the lighthearted scenes, however, for the dark scenes, they stick closer to red and black, perfectly capturing the atmosphere. If there is a fight scene, on the other hand, it goes all out in color. It also helps that the scenes with red and black give some intense, easy to follow and visceral action scenes, especially Bambi saving Faline from the dogs, and Bambi with The Great Prince Of The Forest running from the biggest form of arson the forest has ever suffered from. However, my favorite part is the attention to detail. A deer can barely walk when it is born, runs in strides when it is a foal, and walks like a confident young adult in its adulthood, which is exactly what Bambi does. The same thing applies to every other animal in the picture: they move naturally. This is due to the animators doing their homework to study how woodland critters move. Unlike Snow White And The Seven Dwarves, where the creatures are background props, in this show, the forest animals are the main stars. So the attention to detail must be prominent, and sure enough, it is! VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Our main hero is Bambi. Bambi, as our coming-of-age protagonist, has an exceptional character arc of learning the ways of the forest. Thumper and Flower are his charming best friends, with a lot of flair, I just wish they had a real arc, for example, learning to give Bambi the independence he needs. Faline feels like she is just there. She is crucial to the story, but may feel like a plot device. Same applies to Bambi’s Mother, yet she does have plenty of screen time, making her death hit hard. Man is our antagonist, and he feels so eerie, due to us never even seeing him, meaning he could be one of us. The Great Prince Of The Forest is iconic, however, as he feels like he is crucial to the story, without feeling like a plot device. To conclude, the characters have a lot of charm, but they could feel more like actual people, meaning I would like an arc from them. VERDICT: 14/20

# Acting

# Donnie Dunagan and Hardie Albright were Bambi, and they sold the naivety turned confidence exceptionally. Peter Behn and Sam Edwards were Thumper and they had a lot of charm. Sterling Holloway is the same deal as Flower. Cammie King was Faline, with a beautiful voice, along with a real feminine (Good thing here) voice, while Fred Shields as The Great Prince Of The Forest has a bold, deep voice, perfect for Shields’ character.  However, I just wish Paula Winslowe as Bambi’s Mother said her lines like she had already read them before. VERDICT: 18/20

# Music

# The music’s creation was of Frank Churchill and Edward H. Plumb, and they did a great job! They really poured their heart out for the music, to the point where even the soft scenes are incredibly musical heavy, which do not fit well. However, since they always make it heavy, so since most of the scenes are heavy, it works in the favor! For example: Bambi’s Mother’s death goes hard, with the music, because it should! VERDICT: 16/20

# To conclude, they say that this film has stood the test of time. I do seem to agree, as while yes, some elements are flawed, however, there is way more good than harm done, making this an awesome film. I first watched this, when my younger brother became a toddler, and did not enjoy it very much. However, in terms of my opinion, it has not only stood the test of time, but actually gotten better! Final Score: 88%
