# **Rio (2011)**. I did notice a pattern with movies of Blue Sky studios at the time of its release. Ice Age movie, then an original picture, Ice Age movie, then an original picture, Ice Age movie, then an original picture, and so on. But anyways, back upon the film’s production, this movie’s idea by Carlos Saldanha started in 1995, and was about a penguin, washed upon the beaches of Rio. But since Happy Feet (2006) and Surf’s Up (2007) were released before it, he needed to change the script. Until, he decided to work for Blue Sky studios with Christopher Wedge in order to make Rio. For the film’s release, they would go to Rio, and study both the city, and the Spix’s Macaw, a critically endangered species, in order to bring the film to life. The writers used their script to prove that trading these, or any birds was illegal, and with that, the budget was $90 million. For the music composer, John Powell was hired to collaborate with Sergio Mendes, and the film flew into theaters, grossing 484 million and 600,000 dollars. But is this film as beautiful as finding an endangered species, or just another flightless film? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# At first glance, the story is supposed to feel grand as the *Carnival do Brasil*, as the film takes place in Brazil, and has an epic scope. Unfortunately, the film just lives up to quite little to raise my expectations. The film, instead aspires to be a Point A to Point B generic story, which really lets me down. First off, we have had so much of these Point A to Point B stories, and this film does next to nothing to give it that much of a vibe. Second, it does everything predictable you would expect. It has a cute, yet generic romance, Blu learning to fly in the last minute, and a scary villain Nigel, hunting him down. It does not help that the comedy is anything but hilarious. Now, the comedy gets a chuckle out of me on average, but I never laughed that loud. I did like and relate to the message of being aware of animal’s conservation, but I just wish that the pacing could be slow enough to focus on it. However, I do have quite a lot about the story that I actually liked. We keep spending time with Blu and Jewel, which makes the heart between them feel real and powerful. I also think that the charm is still there with a lot of care for the audience. **VERDICT: 10/20**

# **Animation**

# Now, while I had quite some criticisms for the story, but for the animation? It flies as high as any bird can, and I love it so much, as I feel like this was what the picture tried the hardest with. First off, I love the character designs and rendering. They look like actual humans and the rendering reflects what they should look like perfectly. I also love the action scene in the finale. You never know what is going to happen in those scenes, and the action choreography does not disappoint. The character animation is equally impressive, as the characters move so fluidly, and accurate to their species. But I see you thinking me to write a negative review on the cinematography. But, maybe check for another review, as it looks absolutely gorgeous, due to it depicting Rio in a way that is so grand. But the color is my favorite aspect. The film is so beautiful and colorful across the rainbow. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Characters**

# The film’s cast of characters is… good enough. The human characters are something to be desired, but the animal characters are iconic. Blu is our main protagonist, and Tyler Blu Gunderson has a clear internal motive: to return home. Yes, he is somewhat of a generic protagonist, but his character arc is very well paced. Jewel is the bird Blu is literally chained up with, and she has a real attitude, yet her sensitivity presents her as a more complex character than she seems. Nico, Pedro y Rafael are the eccentric trio who help out the macaws. What I love about the three is that they have a lot of life put into them. You really find them as full of life, entertaining and eccentric. Luís is full of life as well, and while he had no purpose to the story, he was pretty funny. Marcel, Tipa y Armando are the three antagonists, and they suck. They have no character traits, just attempts to be necessary for the story. It backfires, because they are barely in the movie. Linda and Túlio are the characters who are looking for Blu, and that just does not work, because there already is a generic story. We do not need two of them! Finally, Nigel is the antagonist. Nigel is a terrifying and actually unique villain, because he wants to help kill his own kind, because another replaced him, and ate a drumstick of an animal that was his own kind. That is creepy. **VERDICT: 14/20**

# **Acting**

# Jesse Eisenberg is Blu. Eisenberg is well known for being fast-talking, charming and charasmatic. These traits affect Blu’s personality perfectly, due to his terrified nature. Anne Hathaway is Jewel, and Hathaway portrays her character with such elegance and charisma. You still buy Jewel’s attitude, but you also feel her kindness. Jamie Foxx, George Lopez and will.i.am are Nico, Pedro y Rafael. The three have such distinct personalities, but are all blasts to watch. Leslie Mann and Rodrigo Santoro are Linda and Tulio and they have such excellent chemistry with each other, and everyone else. Tracy Morgan is Luis. Morgan really has this charm in his voice. Yet he still feels so charismatic and Morgan’s voice makes you want him to have a happy ending. Marcel, Tipa y Armando are played by Jeffrey Garcia, Carlos Ponce and Davi Vieira, who do not sell it with the fear factor, but they at least have their comedic moments. Finally, Nigel is brought to life with striking ambition and charisma by Jemaine Clement. **VERDICT: 18/20**

# **Music**

# The music here is great, with my only criticism being that I wish the score is… decent at best. It has a Brazilian theme, but you get next to nothing else that really stands out. But worry not, the songs are fantastic! "Real in Rio" presents how massive their world is, and perfectly establishes the epic scope of the world they live in. "Hot Wings (I Wanna Party)" out of the songs is the most fun, and really gets what this movie is supposed to be., with "Hot Wings (I Wanna Party)" being a runner-up for it. You then have "Fly Love", and AYOO! But on the more appropriate side, you got "Pretty Bird", which is the terrifying. But my favorite song in the movie is unequivocally "Mas Que Nada". I sung this song in chorus, and thought it perfectly established the world that these characters live in. **VERDICT: 16/20**

# **In conclusion**, this movie is an incredibly flawed, yet fun time. Rio (2011) is a movie, that in spite of its not great story, has likable characters, masterful animation, great and catchy music and near-flawless acting. It is a movie that I would recommend to any animal fans, or fans of Blue Sky, but if not, then you may watch this less than multiple times. **Final Score: 78%**
