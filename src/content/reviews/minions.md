# Minions. For Despicable Me’s 5th year anniversary, the charming critters had their prequel on how they met Gru. As the first non-Disney animated film to hit a billion, this film is nowhere near as acclaimed as Despicable Me 1, back in 2010. But is this an underrated gem, or just dumb? SPOILERS!!!

# Story

# The premise of the story could have been: how the minions met Gru and became his henchmen. Yeah, that never happened. How so? Because this is a Minions movie, so it must be full of annoying antics. For me, I find the story aimless and episodic. I feel that it is just one Minion shenanigan after another. Nothing more. I find the plot lazy and exhausting. Yes, the comedy can get a chuckle out of me, and the heart is pretty prominent. But I still see the story as a missed opportunity. VERDICT: 6/20

# Animation

# Just to get the negatives out the way, first, this film gives off pretty stupid character designs for a 3D computer-animated movie. But make no mistake: the character animation looks… pretty good. I also love how the colors lean more towards Red and Yellow, which looks like it has more of an identity than most other animated movies of 2015. But oh my god, the action scenes look fantastic! VERDICT: 16/20

# Characters

# I have no like (except for Little Gru), and yet no dislike either. The minions of Kevin, Bob and Stuart as the Minions are charming, yet have no character traits, nor qualms. Scarlett Overkill is humorous, and yet a horrible threat. Same thing with Herb, but at least he has funny one-liners. The Nelsons have no story purpose, but they are pretty charming and likable, despite being criminals. The narrator has the best one-liners, with the least story purpose, and necessity to be here. Why did Gru not tell the story? Queen Elizabeth II starts off with no story purpose, and ends up with plenty. To conclude, there are more characters than what the story needed, but they still can be funny. VERDICT: 10/20

# Acting

# Pierre Coffin plays the Minions, with his signature charm and comedy. It works here. Sandra Bullock as Scarlett Overkill does not, however. She tries so hard to be campy, that it fails miserably. Same applies to Jon Hamm as Herbert Overkill, her husband. Michael Beattie, Allison Janna and Katy Mixon as the Nelsons are terribly humorous, when as criminals, they should be fearful! Michael Keaton (A pretty good actor) does not understand his role here. Even Geoffrey Rush as the Narrator doesn’t suck, and yet I prefer Bill Nighy. And finally, Jennifer Saunders as Queen Elizabeth II acts like a singer, and yet she should be majestic, due to her character being iconic for that! VERDICT: 4/20

# Music

# This film was rarely ever has the Despicable Me theme, but whenever it does, I have not the feeling it belongs in the film at this exact scene. Instead, they just try to cram in 100 villain themes. The result? Where did we go wrong? But, “Happy Together” by the Turtles most certainly did not belong in the opening. My least favorite part, however? The main theme of this film is charming, sure, but also adventurous, and this film is not an adventure! VERDICT: 3/20

# To conclude, I dislike this movie pretty much. I do see the potential, but too bad the overall project fell flat. Final Score: 37%
