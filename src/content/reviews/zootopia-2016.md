# **Zootopia (2016)**. For Disney’s 55th animated movie, they wanted to return to the world of talking animals. The film is about Officer Judy Hopps, who gets recruited and asked by Mrs. Otterton to find her husband, Emmet. She teams up with con artist, Nick Wilde to find Mr. Otterton, and figure out why 14 mammal cases are all predators. The backstory was absolutely crazy. It was a metropolis, referred to as “Zistopia” where Predators were to wear collars. If they got too excited when prey was nearby, the collars would shock them. Nick wanted to use an underground facility illegally, in order to keep predators from said harm. But, since it was too dark, they completely changed the premise. And thank god that they did! This movie today has an 8.0/10 on IMDb, and Rotten Tomatoes gave it a 98%. But does it deserve such praise? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# The story’s message is never to judge a book by its cover, and despite your limitations, you are capable. At first, it is a buddy comedy, about a passionate bunny officer and a naughty fox teaming up together. I was relatively surprised on how slow the pacing was. The movie clocks in at 1 hour and 49 minutes, making it one of Disney’s films that takes its time, the most. There are plenty of chase scenes, in order to make the action scenes exciting, but they slow the movie down, in order to make the case exciting Judy’s and Nick’s character arcs feel earned. But as the movie goes on, the film gets even better. The film starts to be about prejudice and bigotry. I love these themes, because they invite adults into the movie’s conversation, all while still being fun for children. I was actually surprised that any animated film would be brave enough to talk about politics, even if it cared. Thankfully, Disney tried it, and I love these themes risk it takes. Also, treating prey like white people and predators as black people is a perfect and very real and relatable metaphor. This makes it released at the perfect time. As candidates for the President of 2017 used fear for votes. The other half explores everything else very well, such as how the predator and prey act like humans, and their history. My only real gripe are the outdated references. **VERDICT: 18/20**

# **Animation**

# I already stated how I loved how the movie explored everything with the story. This also applies to the animation, because this is one of the most gorgeous animated features of all time. Right off, this is some of animation’s greatest background imagery ever created. It really looks like Disney valued every area of the city, and used the parts to their full advantage, in order to present how these animals live in their respective part of the city. That, and there is a lot of attention to detail, such as the tubes the hamsters slide down on. This does not just look like a normal city with  anthropomorphic animals. Every single element has been thought out. But the character designs and animation are also excellent. Not only do the animals look part like their animals, part like humans, making them feel anthropomorphic, but the character animation also goes a very long way. It is both smooth, and elegant, which is hard to do. Each animal has their own features. From how the elephants use their trunks, to how Judy shows her fear, by lifting her ears. The list goes on! With all of that said, imagine how the action scenes would look like! Pretty amazing, I will tell you that much. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Characters**

# The cast of characters here is done well, but I must refer to them as the weakest aspects of the movie. Not that they are bad, just that they could be even better. You begin with Officer Judith “Judy” Hopps, one of the greatest protagonists in all of Disney. She undergoes an arc of learning how to realize how life is not perfect. Her character arc is so slow paced, and she actually struggles for a lot of the movie. But since she gets better and better, it feels earned. Nicholas J. “Nick” Wilde is her partner. Not only do I appreciate how they never even have an “I love you” across the entire movie, but he also becomes more honest of a character. And for example, when he stands up for Judy, he becomes way more selfless. However, while the police force start to respect Judy more, and while they do have different personalities, which make the world feel lived in, they could have more story purpose. In fact, you could say that with most of the side characters. Yes, characters like Mr. Big, Flash, Lionheart, and Judy’s parents have the most of a vibe, but they could have more of a story purpose. The villain, Bellwether, however, is my least favorite. She just changes personality the moment she becomes the villain, and due to her being so nice in the first place gets you very suspicious. **VERDICT: 15/20**

# **Acting**

# The acting is a big improvement from the characters, as I actually loved the actors very much. Ginnifer Goodwin is Judy, and Goodwin really cares about making Judy likable, yet acknowledges her struggles and more heartfelt moments. Jason Bateman is Nick Wilde. Bateman sounds so hilariously sarcastic, but actually cares for the slower moments. What I love about the two actors is that they care about their characters. The police force are most notably played by Idris Elba and Nate Torrence, respectively. The side characters have such differences from each other’s voices, such as Bonnie Hunt, Don Lake and Raymond S. Persi. Then for the villains, you have J.K. Simmons and Jenny Slate are the antagonists. Simmons does a good job with his deliveries, but Slate sucks, with sounding even remotely sinister. But if you can get past that, then the cast is amazing. **VERDICT: 19/20**

# **Music**

# I absolutely love the music to an insane degree. You have the main theme, of "Suite from Zootopia". It feels and sounds so authentic, with such attention to the beats. You have the panic themes. For said panic themes, you have "Ramifications", "Hopps Goes (After) the Weasel", and "Case of the Manchas". They sound so different, and actually add on to the tension. The sad and powerful  themes are "The Nick of Time", "World's Worst Animal Shelter" and “A Bunny Can Go Savage" sound like animated cinema, because the amazing score composer, Michael Giacchino. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **To conclude**, Zootopia is a masterpiece. It is an incredible tale, about how you can take risks, in terms of its production, and one of the most powerful animated movies of the decade. I would highly recommend this to any single fan of Disney, except if you are a character perfectionist. It is a masterpiece, that I doubt will be done again, and I have gratitude that this won the sixteenth Oscar for Best Animated Feature. **Final Score: 92%**
