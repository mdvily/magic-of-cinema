**Zootopia (2016)**. For Disney’s 55th animated movie, they wanted to return to the world of talking animals. The film is about Officer Judy Hopps, who gets recruited and asked by Mrs. Otterton to find her husband, Emmet. She teams up with con artist, Nick Wilde to find Mr. Otterton, and figure out why 14 mammal cases are all predators. The backstory was absolutely crazy. It was a metropolis, referred to as “Zistopia” where Predators were to wear collars. If they got too excited when prey was nearby, the collars would shock them. Nick wanted to use an underground facility illegally, in order to keep predators from said harm. But, since it was too dark, they completely changed the premise. And thank god that they did! This movie today has an 8.0/10 on IMDb, and Rotten Tomatoes gave it a 98%. But does it deserve such praise? **SPOILERS!**

**Story**

The story’s message is never to judge a book by its cover, and despite your limitations, you are capable. At first, it is a buddy comedy, about a passionate bunny officer and a naughty fox teaming up together. I was relatively surprised on how slow the pacing was. The movie clocks in at 1 hour and 49 minutes, making it one of Disney’s films that takes its time, the most. There are plenty of chase scenes, in order to make the action scenes exciting, but they slow the movie down, in order to make the case exciting Judy’s and Nick’s character arcs feel earned. But as the movie goes on, the film gets even better. The film starts to be about prejudice and bigotry. I love these themes, because they invite adults into the movie’s conversation, all while still being fun for children. I was actually surprised that any animated film would be brave enough to talk about politics, even if it cared. Thankfully, Disney tried it, and I love these themes risk it takes. Also, treating prey like white people and predators as black people is a perfect and very real and relatable metaphor. This makes it released at the perfect time. As candidates for the President of 2017 used fear for votes. The other half explores everything else very well, such as how the predator and prey act like humans, and their history. My only real gripe are the outdated references. **VERDICT: 18/20**
