# Mary Poppins. What do you expect from a 1964 musical about a fairy? How about a whimsical, charming and magical tale? Yes, this is one of the most acclaimed family movies of the 60’s! People are saying that this film has stood the test of time. But is this “masterpiece” for real? Let us find out! SPOILERS!

# Story

# The story, based on the books is simple; A fairy comes to the Banks family household to babysit the children, Michael and Jane, and teaches the profound lessons of fun and kindness. This film brings the F in Fun. There re so many musical numbers that fit the story and tone perfectly! The comedy is humorous, with plenty of jokes and charm. All while, the movie has plenty of heart, that makes this film an excellent moment, especially if you are not in the best mood. My only real gripe? It never explains how Mary Poppins received her powers? Did she get them, or was she biologically born with them? The feature never explains it. VERDICT: 19/20

# Visuals

# This film looks beautiful, to the point where it would look gorgeous, no matter if it were live-action, or any kind of animation. That is how well it was executed! First off, the colors are gorgeous! The film leads into color blue, perfectly affecting the whimsy! I also love the cinematography. The film leads more into establishing shots, than anything else, perfectly capturing the occasionally mysterious tone. But whenever the characters go into the animation, I love the creativity of it! Lastly, and definitely mostly, the choreography is eccentric and all over the place. And you know what? The choreography looks better than West Side Story (1961)! VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Bert is Poppins’ best friend, and he is a charming jack-of-all-trades, making him less predicable. The Poppins children of Michael and Jane are character POVs at their best. You really… feel what they feel about Poppins. The Banks mother and father are the perfect catalysts to the story. However, can you really discuss the fact that the cast is incredible without mentioning Mary Poppins herself? I think not! Mary is a charming, and lovely babysitter, perfectly giving the film whimsy and charm. VERDICT: 20/20

# Acting

# Reta Shaw and Hermione Baddeley are the staff, and they sell the exhaustion of the maid & cook. Glynis Johns and David Tomlinson are the Banks parents. The mother sells it at getting distracted, and the father demands perfection and precision in the Banks household. Matthew Garber and Karen Dotrice are the children Michael Banks and Jane Banks. Dick Van Dyke as Bert is charming, and has amazing chemistry to Julie Andrews’ Mary Poppins, who kills it in terms of both singing and dialogue. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# “A Spoonful of Sugar” is a happy musical number with an incredible theme: Any task can be enjoyable. “Jolly Holiday” and "Supercalifragilisticexpialidocious" look beautiful, due to the animation perfectly affecting the tone of the story. "Feed the Birds" is a peaceful tune. "Chim Chim Cher-ee" won the Oscar for “Best Original Song” and with its lyrics, deserves it! "Step in Time" is an exhilarating song, sung by the right characters, and lastly, "Let's Go Fly a Kite" is the emotional finale, and the perfect message delivery: to take seriously the meaning of family. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, Mary Poppins is a masterful movie and wonderful musical! Maybe, not perfect, but it never falters in terms of working exceptionally. As the first movie I saw in theaters for an anniversary, this is one of my favorites, in terms of writing, visuals, acting and songs. Final Score: 99%
