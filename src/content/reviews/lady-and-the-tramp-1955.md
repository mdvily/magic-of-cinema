**Lady And The Tramp (1955)**. In the year of 1937, when Disney’s company started releasing films, Joe Grant had a new child of his own. Unfortunately, his cocker spaniel dog was upset. The dog felt like it had been replaced. Grant sent this news public, and Disney used this as an idea for his Studio’s 15th movie. As the the film released as of today, 71 years and 3 days ago, fans disapproved of it, even calling it a bad movie. However, in recent years, the movie has gotten a reevaluation. Fans even calling this a classic, to this day! But is this a good dog, or is this as bad as it was all those years ago? **SPOILERS!**

**Story**

After watching this movie for the first time, I disliked it quite a bit. A reason is because I found the movie predictable, and the pacing slow. I still find the movie predictable. How so? Lady and Tramp get curious of each other. This happened a lot, even at the time. They get separated. This happened a lot, even at the time. They end up together, a family of their own. This happened a lot, even at the time. You start to notice how the movie did not take many risks. It keeps the film from having a real vibe. Also, the comedy is hit or miss. I never wanted to stop watching the film, because of its jokes, but I never really laughed a lot. I just… chuckled at best, and rolled my eyes at worst. This is because I do not approve of its cartoonish slapstick that much. But if you can get past that, then this film has a great story.
