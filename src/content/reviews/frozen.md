# Frozen. Disney had wanted to make an animated picture of Hans Christian Andersen’s “The Snow Queen” in 1844. In fact, as a film supposed to release in 1943, as the canon’s sixth ever movie. However, it went through production troubles, with recasting, changing the story and making the animation look different, for 7 decades. But, when it became Disney’s 53rd animated feature, in 2013, fans and critics alike loved this movie. Even though, 13 years after its release, it has been considered overrated. But is this a frozen fever dream, or royally rambunctious?

# Story

# Now, Disney has a strict fairy tale subversion formula, featuring true love, an innocent hero and a wicked for the love of the game villain. And yet, Frozen changes the tale. Most Disney movies are about true love, however this film is about the love of two sisters. This helps with the heart, so much, due to them being different. The comedy is still both there and humorous, and the theme of sibling hood is jawdropping. My only critique: The plot twists can be predictable. VERDICT: 19/20

# Animation

# Right off the bat, the action scenes, such as the wolf chase are very intense, and the character animations look realistic for humans, but they go all out when it dedicates itself to the non-humans. From Sven’s goofy expressions, to Olaf’s deconstructing and reconstructing. My only criticism is the character designs. They look far from bad, but they look a bit too much like Tangled. However, the backgrounds of Arendelle, and the special effects of snow popping out from Elsa’s abilities are a true spectacle. VERDICT: 19/20

# Characters

# These characters are nothing short of amazing. Elsa, as our protagonist, is one of Disney’s best, along with Simba (in the 1994 film) and Beast. She wants things to be how they once were, and yet she still is worried about what could happen, making her very relatable and complex. Anna is quirky, and you feel her worries for her elder sister. Kristoff and Sven are humorous, and their character development of kindness and true love is amazing. Elsa’s parents and Pappie are extremely integral to the story. Olaf is humorous, and is an incredible way to be a role model for kids. However, Hans is a predictable villain, with pretty stupid motives. VERDICT: 18/20  

# Acting

# Idina Menzel as Elsa, yet discovering her potential. Kristen Bell as Anna is quirky, yet shy. Jonathan Groff as Kristoff is smart, yet arrogant. Santino Fontana as Hans is so charming, yet scary in the end, you forget about his flaws, in terms of writing! Ciaran Hinds as Pappie is wise, and trustworthy. However, there will never be another comic relief portrayal, like Josh Gad as Olaf. He is so humorous, yet charming. To conclude, the actors and actresses are so good at their roles, that I forgot they were acting! VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# “Frozen Heart” is the attempted theme song to the film. It only failed, because there was an even better song in the picture. “Do You Want to Build a Snowman?” is a perfect scene for Anna to try to connect to her older sister. “For the First Time in Forever“ shows Anna’s excitement for the gates to finally be opened. “Love Is an Open Door” is the most important song to Anna’s character. “Reindeer(s) Are Better Than People” is Kristoff’s humorous song. “In Summer” is funny, and perfectly shows Olaf’s plot? “For the First Time in Forever (Reprise)” is satisfying for both the story and Elsa and Anna’s characters. “Fixer Upper” is even more so. But “Let It Go” is one of the most iconic songs to ever be in a movies, for all of the right reasons. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, Frozen is one of the most incredible movies Disney has made in the 2010 decade. It has a near thought-provoking story, breathtaking animation, a phenomenal cast of characters, incredible acting and amazing music. Final Score: 96%
