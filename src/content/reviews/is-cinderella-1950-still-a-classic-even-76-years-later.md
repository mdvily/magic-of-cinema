# **Cinderella (1950)**. Once upon a time, Walter “Walt” Disney became a famous man 103 years ago. In 1923, Disney made his studio referred to as Walt Disney Pictures. For the golden age of their movies, they did pretty well, in terms of quality. Even if the box office could be way better. To the point where the wartime era, that being Saludos Amigos (1942), The Three Caballeros (1944), Make Mine Music (1946), Fun and Fancy Free (1947), Melody Time (1948) and The Adventures of Ichabod and Mr. Toad (1949) nearly ruined the studio. The box office and quality was so disappointing, compared to the Golden Age, that the studio near shut down. And then there was Cinderella (1950). **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# The story deviates vastly from the 1812 fairy tale from the Brothers Grimm, in order to have more Disney magic, such as the ball being one night only, or Cinderella only living happily ever after, rather than punishing her step-family. I would say it works, since it makes the film more age appropriate. The story can be predictable at times. Watching it, I knew everything that was going to happen, because the movie does very little to set itself apart. That, and the story structure is borderline garbage. We see more of the planning of the ball, and the mice Jaq, Gus and Luke running away from the cat Lucifer, than we do with Cinderella’s story. I also felt that the romance was underdeveloped. We never find out the details of what Cinderella or Prince Charming think of each other. But get past that, and I like the story quite a bit. The comedy is hilarious. Usually, I hate slapstick in movies, but I think we can make an exception here. This is because I actually laughed, and found the comedy a reason why the mice are here. Then, there is the heart and drama. I actually cried every scene where Cinderella got abused by her stepmother, and stepsisters. The film would consistently take its time to showcase these scenes. And the mice always being there for Cinderella is sweet, because she always returns the favor. **VERDICT: 15/20**

# **Animation**

# The animation is an absolute spectacle. The movie has this look that ages incredibly well, because they knew how to make it work. First off, the color of the backgrounds and clothing the characters wear perfectly affects the mood. For example, the scene where we first see Cinderella with Lady Tremaine, the room is dark, but when she meets the Fairy Godmother, the scene is light. This perfectly affects the scene. Also, Cinderella’s light clothes perfectly affect her kind demeanor, and you can tell that Lady Tremaine is cruel, because she dark purple and normal red clothes. The red is her hatred for her stepdaughter, and the purple is her cruelty. The character designs and animation is also great. I found the character animation very fluid, and the designs very well representing the character personalities, all while being very accurate to real life. There are no action scenes, but there is a choreography scene. That scene is the ball. The scene has such beautiful choreography, that I had no idea this was a movie. I thought it was real! **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Characters**

# The characters of Cinderella are iconic, but the writing of them, even if it works, can undeniably be improved on. Cinderella is our main protagonist. She wants more with her life, and to escape her stepfamily’s physical and mental abuse. I get the gist of her motive, and can relate. We all have aspirations, even if we all have obstacles holding us back. However, she is merely a POV in a movie that needs her to be more. The events just happen around her, rather than her doing anything to drive the story forward. Then you have Prince Charming. He is her love interest, but is my least favorite character. This is because he never has any character. Yes, he is in love with the young woman, but we never see another character trait from him. Now, onto the good characters! The mice Jaq, Gus and Luke are the companions of Cinderella. Them being mice gives them a vibe, and I love their kindness to Cinderella. Whenever the mice work together to help Cinderella, I actually cried. Lady Tremaine, Anastasia and Drizella are the antagonists. They have such a relationship to her, and their personalities are very real. I just wish we knew why they hated Cinderella. And then, you have The Fairy Godmother. She is an eccentric fairy, who helps Cinderella to win the prince over at the dance. Out of every character in this movie, I find her the most great at being important to the story. However, her personality gives her a real vibe. **VERDICT: 15/20**

# **Acting**

# While the character writing could be improved upon, the acting just cannot. Ilene Woods is Cinderella. On the surface, Woods is so lovely and kind to others, but deep down, you can hear the vulnerability in her voice. William Phipps is Prince Charming, who really understands his character, and has the chemistry with Woods. Rhoda Williams and Lucille Bliss are Drizella and Anastasia. The two are really annoying, but who knows? Maybe that is a good thing, because that is what Cinderella sees her stepsisters as. James MacDonald is the mice. I love how he sounds so squeaky, and his chemistry with the other mice. Verna Felton is The Fairy Godmother. Now, most of the time, Felton is loud and boisterous, so since she has a cool and calm voice, she is so different from her other roles. I love it. Lastly, Eleanor Audley is Lady Tremaine. She has such a quiet and chilling voice for most of the movie, so when she gets loud, it feels real. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **Music**

# The music is the final and my personal favorite aspect of the film is the music. The film has a more waltz-y feel to it, and it works so well for the movie’s favor. All of the six original tracks are my least to most favorite is a hard task, because I love all of them. “Oh, Sing Sweet Nightingale” is Anastasia teaching Cinderella how life is just cruel, and she has no hope. Yes, that is but the truth, but you have to remember, that is the point. That is what Cinderella is supposed to think, so that a happy and earned scene can happen, and be brought to life by a perfect catalyst, of this song. “The Work Song (Cinderella)” is the mice finally making the most out of their story purpose, by making Cinderella a dress. There is no magic, just hard work that feels earned- oh, never mind. Tremaine’s cruel daughters ruin their stepsister’s dress. But then, you have the song, of “Bibbidi-Bobbidi-Boo (The Magic Song)”. I needed it as much as Schindler’s List (1993)’s audience needed the shower scene. It feels so light and cheerful to the audience, and it feels earned, like a breath of fresh air, after how hard it was to swallow the pill of Cinderella’s dress vandalism. Now the next three were incredibly hard to rank, because I feel like they rival the songs of Singin’ In The Rain (1952). In third place is what I feel is the most important song in the story. “So This Is Love” is when Cinderella finally meets The Prince, and Cindy’s life is changed forever. It just sounds so beautiful, like what I want to hear when I come back to life. “A Dream Is a Wish Your Heart Makes” is the main song of Disney’s sweetest Princess, nay character. It just had to have incredible vocals from Ilene Woods, and it just does. But my favorite song of them all is “Cinderella”. This is the main theme of the movie, and the lyrics are otherworldly. “Cinderella, if you give your heart a chance, It will lead you to the kingdom of romance” is perfect for this story, and the incredible message. **VERDICT: 20/20**

# **In conclusion**, Cinderella (1950) is an absolute masterpiece of a movie. The film feels so rich and robust as a movie, even with some of its writing shortcomings. It is also a film that I obligate Princess fans, and children with unfortunate situations to watch at least once in their life. But the real reason on why I love this movie so much, is because of its legacy to Disney cinema. After the box office bombs that were all six Disney Wartime Era movies, Disney was in hot water. Cinderella had to be a hit, with Walt Disney even saying to his “boys”, “If Cinderella doesn’t make it, we’re through!” And the result? Cinderella just did make it. Because of this, the studio lived on, and therefore its legacy is very important to Disney’s timeline, in terms of the studio’s big hits and misses. This was an essential hit. **Final Score: 90%**
