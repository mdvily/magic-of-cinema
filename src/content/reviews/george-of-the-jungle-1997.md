# **George Of The Jungle (1997)**. 1967‘s George Of The Jungle show is considered to be a love letter to Tarzan, to the point where they made a movie about it. The original idea was a jungle man who was dumped by his wealthy parents. That, and George's physical appearance and characterization were inspired by the physical nature and demeanor of George Eiferman, a real-life bodybuilder and Mr. America, or Mr. Universe winner. That was for the show, and for the movie, the budget was only for 55 million dollars. The film would also have the narrator break the fourth wall to tell the audience who these characters are. And for the show’s 30th year anniversary, a movie was bound to come out. A not very acclaimed one, mind you. The critics on rotten tomatoes gave it 56%, and audiences gave it a 42%. So, I just have to ask, did we all watch the same movie? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# Right off the bat, the story takes liberties from the show, by not just making it fun. It gives it an actual story. Said story is George and Ursula falling in love, as George along with his main animal friends Ape, Shep and Tookie teach Ursula the ways of the jungle, all while Ursula’s fiancé, Lyle is hot on her trail. Now, the story can be very stupid at times. And I mean, very stupid. Ursula and George could have way more character development as a duo. This makes the romance weak, because they only fight to be funny for the audience. That, and if Ursula has Lyle, but George is kinder, why not just break up? But you have not a reason to worry, for everything else works. First off, the heart is in the right place. George keeps showing unconditional kindness to the animals, and I relate to that very much. I love animals! But near the end of the movie, when the poachers kidnap Ape, I did actually tear up. We spend so much time with the two, that I actually cared. But this is a comedy, so there are plenty of jokes. And you know what? I laughed at all of them! I found the entire movie hilarious, and the jokes themselves are made up of slapstick and one liners that fit the tone, perfectly. To conclude, the romance is a weak point, but everything else works. **VERDICT: 16/20**

# **Visuals**

# This movie is a near treat to look at, and I have not a lot to complain about. My only real gripe is that the animal designs look… pretty awful, sometimes. They should have brought and trained more animals, because whenever they do it, it looks pretty good. But aside from that, the visuals are really good. The color palette is mostly green and brown. First hearing that, I thought it would look ugly, but no! The movie looks beautiful and like a habitat I would love to visit on a vacation. Then, there is the lighting. The lighting is generally bright, which welcomes the viewer into their world. I always felt like this was a paradise. But the city of San Francisco is also well represented, in terms of lighting. But my favorite aspect of the visuals is the cinematography. It makes every shot look brought to life, and done so in the most gorgeous way. Other than my one criticism, I love everything else about the visuals! **VERDICT: 17/20**

# **Characters**

# The characters on average are likable, and you enjoy them, but they could have had more of a character arc. George of the Jungle is our protagonist. George is very likable, and you buy his good nature. The problem is that he goes from being a likable character to a likable character. This stops him from having a real character arc. Ursula, his girlfriend, is charming, and shows how girl power is a thing. But she, unlike George, has a real character arc. She learns to not let Lyle affect her as a person. Ape, Shep and Tookie are George’s companions. I love how they have different personalities. Ape is an intelligent ape, who teaches George a lot that he knows. Shep thinks he is a dog, and acts like one pretty well. Tookie is the eye in the sky, known for his signature call. Lyle Van de Groot is the main antagonist. He is funny, yet he is barely in the movie, and I never watch the film for him. Finally, the narrator just explains the story, so you do not have to. That is basically it. **VERDICT: 11/20**

# **Acting**

# Brendan Fraser is George himself. Fraser is incredibly charming and sweet. You really buy him as a loving and caring man. If only he could sell it better in the emotional department. Leslie Mann is Ursula. Mann knows exactly how to make you buy Ursula’s struggles. This is because she sells it in both the comedic and emotional department. John Cleese and Frank Welker did the voices of Ape, Shep and Tookie, and they sell the voices. Not once did I think that they sounded weird, and Tookie, Scooper, and Hopper as Tookie and Tai as Shep did the body movements, and they moved so naturally. Keith Scott is the Narrator. Scott sounds boisterous and powerful the whole time, which is just… of course he did. It gets kind of on my nerves, because that is what they always do. At least The Verminator-I mean Thomas Haden Church puts in a very good portrayal of Lyle. Church sounds so sinister, yet incompetent as Lyle, and that is perfect to his portrayal. **VERDICT: 18/20**

# **Music**

# The music is just… good. I like it. It sounds very funny, and "The Man on the Flying Trapeze" is an actual jam. "George To The Rescue", "Rumble In The Jungle" and "The Little Monkey" are humorous enough, but do sound a tad bit generic. Marc Shaiman, good job, even though you could do a little bit better. You then have "Wipe Out", which is another bop. It plays whenever George and Ape hang out. This is a perfect choice for said scenes, because it sounds appropriate for the scenes. "My Way" is an amazing song. WHAT DID JOHN CLEESE DO WITH IT?! Nothing, but make it sound dumb. I had to deduct points, and a lot of them, because it was just disrespectful to how hard Sinatra’s lyrics were. But to make up for it, "George of the Jungle" is an amazingly funny theme song, that perfectly affects George’s character. **VERDICT: 14/20**

# **In conclusion**, George Of The Jungle (1997) was not once again, a very acclaimed movie, with critics on rotten tomatoes giving it 56%, and audiences giving it a 42%. So, I just have to ask, did we all watch the same movie? No! I personally enjoyed a lot of this movie. Yes, I do have some problems with it, but get past that, and I would recommend it. **Final Score: 76%**
