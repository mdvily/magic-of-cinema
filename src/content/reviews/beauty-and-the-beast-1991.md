# **Beauty And The Beast (1991)**.Once upon a time, Disney animation studios was facing a crisis. The founder of the studio, Walter “Walt” Disney passed away, and the animators crew, the nine old men lived up to their name and retired. Therefore, as a result, the Disney movies quality slipped from great to pretty bad, from The Aristocats (1970) and Oliver and Company (1988). Yes, with The Great Mouse Detective (1986) and The Rescuers (1978), there were some good additions to the Walt Disney Pictures family, but it was not the same. However, Disney did what they did best in terms of storytelling, and new technology into their animation into The Little Mermaid (1989) and The Rescuers Down Under (1990), bringing the studio back to its roots. However, Beauty And The Beast (1991) of the original three Disney Renaissance is considered one of the most acclaimed, with an 8 on IMDb, and a 95% on rotten tomatoes, the film is considered one of the greatest in not just Disney, but all of animation, even being the first ever animated feature to win a Best Picture nomination. But is this a masterpiece as old as time? SPOILERS!

# **Story**

# As an animated feature based on a French Fairy Tale by Gabrielle-Suzanne Barbot de Villeneuve in 1740, the film does what Disney does best: deviating from the tale 251 years before the movie. And sure enough: it works in the movie’s favor! The film, due to doing its own thing, is unpredictable, and has its own identity. A requirement for Disney is anything but to be accurate, but one is to have its own identity, and the movie takes advantage of it! The pacing is noticeably slower than what you would expect from the 29 Disney Animated features that came out before the movie. However, the film uses its slow pacing as a very big strength. Why? It makes the romance between Belle and Beast and the character development of Beast feel earned, because the film takes its time for the elements to happen. Speaking of which, the romance is essential to the pacing, and unlike The Little Mermaid, which came out two years before this film did, is three-sided. We get the desperation from Beast, skepticism of Belle and both from the servants, because the film shows us, making it feel real. The comedy is humorous, yet it feels mostly from pretty funny one-liners, yet the heart is required, due to the plot’s nature, to be the most prominent aspect of the film’s story. And sure enough, it is! The heart is so powerful and impactful, because Belle, the servants and Beast have so much chemistry together, making every petal fall of the rose more impactful. VERDICT: 20/20

# **Animation**

# The film looks absolutely beautiful, to the point where I love it as much as Beast and Belle do in the end. The color pallet mostly takes shades of the color yellow. This is beautiful for the film, because it establishes the romance between Belle and Beast, and the blue main shades in the climax show the sorrow the characters must go through in these scenes. The action scene between Beast and Gaston is basically perfect for the animation. The action is fluid and visceral, yet is brave enough to take short breaks in order to help us, the audience understand what goes on in these scenes. The character designs and animation of the humans look like Disney, and only Disney could make them look like real people and this fluid and beautiful. Beast and the servants’ character designs are the highlights on how the characters could look, as with the Prince’s character design, the animators were not afraid to go all out and take their time. The special effects and lighting also work extremely well, here in order to bring to life the scenes. My personal favorite scene, in terms of special effects and lighting is when Beast and The Servants become human, after Beast and Belle finally fall in love. However, the choreography and animated cinematography looks like only James Baxter, the film’s lead animator could have made this, and Baxter made this the highlight of an eye candy of a film. VERDICT: 20/20

# **Characters**

# The characters in Beauty And The Beast (1991) are absolutely perfectly written, due to them being likable and well written. We begin with Prince Adam/ Beast, a complex protagonist. Beast is an extremely selfish character from the start, but his desire to be human again makes him care for the servants, making him be more in love with Belle, turning him into a better person. His character arc, as I said, is very slow, but that is a good thing, as you feel like the romance is paced well. Belle is one of Disney’s most different princesses from the rest, because her loving to read books gives her such a vibe, and while she is kind to Beast, she never feels one-dimensional, as she can snap back at him, at her visible limit. Her romance of Beast, once again, is so slow, it feels earned, but never boring. Lumiere, Cogsworth and Mrs. Potts are the comedic sidekicks, but they never get annoying. Most side characters from the studio, even the better ones can be annoying at times, but just like the Genie from the next movie, they are exceptions to that rule. There are also a lot of other servants, yet these are the main three, so they get the most focus, with Chip for being adorable. Gaston is a humorous and campy antagonist, and his stupidity makes for a great and pretty funny foil to Belle’s intelligence. However, in the finale, he is presented as way more important to the story and way more threatening, but still feels like the same character. LeFou and D’Arque are henchmen of Gaston, and while we see little of them, they make the most out of what they are given, and are way more important to the story, than you might expect. Finally, Belle’s father, Maurice is the perfect catalyst to the story with chemistry with a lot of love for his daughter, Belle. VERDICT: 20/20

# **Acting**

# Robby Benson is Beast, with such finesse and a change in tone. At the start of the movie, Benson has such an attitude, and callousness to his voice, but in the end, he sounds way more nice to Belle, perfectly reflecting upon Beast’s character. Paige O'Hara as Belle is incredibly sweet, yet knows when to have an attitude, when needed, all while having a beautiful singing voice. Jerry Orbach as Lumiere has such a charming French voice, yet knows when to have a more serious tone. Angela Lansbury as Mrs. Potts has such amazing chemistry and motherly tone with Bradley Pierce as Chip Potts. David Ogden Stiers as Cogsworth sounds more hard on Beast, and far more skeptical of Belle, which perfectly makes sense for his character. Richard White as Gaston is a humorous character, until the third act, where he actually has a threatening presence, and a lot of bold and unique tone. Jesse Corti and Tony Jay as LeFou and Monsieur D’Arque are one threatening and one funny and have such chemistry with White. VERDICT: 20/20

# **Music**

# Every song in the movie is iconic, beautiful and perfect for the story, making all of them welcome additions. "Belle" is the song of that introduced us both to Belle’s village, and Belle as a character. You really buy how due to the princess in yellow being different from the others makes her feel bullied, making her reasonably want more from her life. "Belle (Reprise)", fortunately showcases this to the audience, as you get the “I Want” song, making it feel reasonable. "Gaston" is the villain song in the tavern, and is full of life, and gives us details about Gaston I had not a clue I needed. "Gaston (Reprise)" is the more darker version of the previous song, and it had no purpose here, yet with its lyrics, I still am glad it has made it into the picture. "Be Our Guest" is the eccentric welcoming of Belle into the dinner hall by the servants. It is so full of life and so cheerful and happy, perfectly affecting the film’s overall tone. "Something There" is the rejoicing of how close the Beast and servants are to being back to humans. "The Mob Song" is the violent sounding song of Gaston, LeFou and the villagers getting ready to kill Beast. Due to the film’s lighthearted tone, you would be hard pressed to know that this song is even in the movie. This gives it an unpredictability, but it makes sense, due to Gaston’s stupidity. "Prologue: Beauty and the Beast", "The Beast Lets Belle Go" and "Transformation" are my three favorite music tracks, as they could have just been iconic. Fortunately, this is not a trap that it falls into, because they sound like they are important to the story, and the instrumentals go hard, as how they should. "Beauty and the Beast" and "Beauty and the Beast (Duet)" however, are my favorites. A story like this had to feature these songs, and for good reason. They are the story’s message, wrapped into one song, with perfect lyrics. VERDICT: 20/20

# **To conclude,** Beauty And The Beast (1991) is an absolute triumph of a movie, that may be one of Animation’s greatest of all time. The film took The Little Mermaid’s formula, and basically perfected it. If you are a fan of any movie at all, then you actually must watch this movie, no matter what, as this is one of the most iconic, ever. Final Score: 100%
