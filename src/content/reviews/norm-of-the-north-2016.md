**Norm Of The North (2016)**. The movie is a collaboration between Assemblage Entertainment and Splash Entertainment, who are most well known for Alpha and Omega. I have no idea, what either studio was thinking, as to release it on to the big screen, but it completely backfired. The movie has a 3.4/10 on IMDb, and a Rotten Tomatoes score of only 6%, so the audiences hated it to death. But is this film an underrated masterpiece we are not… Nope, this movie is the epitome of an abysmal movie, in every possible way! But what makes it so bad? SPOILERS!

**Story**

This movie has no story to tell, as instead all it has is an incredibly hammered down on us every 5 minutes, of “Condos must stay out of the arctic”. It tries to explain that we should never take from the animals’ habitats to make our own. And why would we do that in the first place, I have no clue, and I do not even care to answer, because the message could have been something good. Instead, it completely sucks, because the film gives us reminders of how the message, like we are stupid, or some garbage like that. However, there are three things that make the film go from unforgivable, to making me want to be put out of my misery. First, there are so many plot cliches that make the film intolerable. For example, the villain wants money and nothing else. Second, the action scenes are everywhere that have no purpose to the plot, if you can even call it that here. And third, the jokes are so bad, so bottom of the barrel, that I forgot what a good joke was for months. The only jokes there are, is Norm, as the moron that he is, forgetting his important mission! All of the story aspects that could have been awful, but this makes them unpredictably unforgiving. Seriously, did anyone even care about this horrendous piece of garbage? **VERDICT: 0/20**

**Animation**

The animation is the biggest reason to ask the question: how did THIS film possibly even come close to making it into theaters, or even on digital? This animation looks like it was supposed to be in a DVD Disney sequel, not a feature film! The animation looks so ugly, especially in the character designs and animation! Seriously, the characters move like mud figures, which is just unacceptable in a movie, that undeservedly hit the big screen. And also, the textures, even New York City, look like a toddler had a blank, white piece of paper, and not only sucks, it brings down the quality of the color as well! The action scenes look so confused and ugly, and the choreography? Can you even call it that? The characters dance like this was their first class, and the only choreography you will even find is Norm twerking to annoyingly frustrating songs! Seriously, the animation looks so bad, I kind of wanted the arctic in this film to melt, forever. **VERDICT: 0/20**

**Characters**

Now that we have gotten the absolute abysmal out of the way, we get into even worse lands. The characters are one-dimensional, bland, and unforgivingly stupid. Norm, our protagonist, is a polar bear, who wants to save the arctic, but the problem is that the film explains nothing about him. How does he know how to communicate with humans, and no other animal does, why is he in love with a human, why is he after his grandfather, and not his father, who knows? Instead, the movie uses all of its next to nonexistent comic reliefs of the lemmings, who are minion ripoffs, who make the actual minions transcendently hilarious in comparison, and I barely laugh that loud at them in the first place. Socrates, Norm’s advisor, could have made wise sentences, yet he never does, because all he ever does is remind Norm to save the arctic, which he should know already! Olivia and Vera are extremely annoying, and intolerable, because they get attracted to Norm for no reason, and have no real purpose to the story. Mr. Greene is the villain, and one of the worst in animation’s history! Why? He cares more about putting condos in the arctic for no reason, and being over the top, but not in a campy or fun way, instead of being a character! But the most hatable characters are the offensive extras in New York, who think Norm is an actual Bear, and are cool with it! I cannot believe how such a big cast of idiots even exists! **VERDICT: 0/20**

**Acting**

Now, I do somewhat feel bad hating the acting so much, as the actors are very talented, on average. Well, this movie is the worst possible example of that, for all of them, because they all suck! Rob Schneider portrays the “lovable” polar bear, Norm. Schneider underacts so much, and never cares that he even is in a movie! Ken Jeong is Mr. Greene, and I just have to say one thing: Mr. Jeong, you are a good actor, but man, you were way better as a doctor, because you have no presence, no fear factor, nothing! Heather Graham and Maya Kay as Vera and Olympia have such terrible chemistry, and never come off as endearing to Schneider’s Norm. Bill Nighy, actually a very talented actor certainly does not show it as Socrates, who does everything to rip off Rowan “Mr. Bean” Atkinson’s Zazu, without even trying to use his own, amazing voice. Gabriel Iglesias and Colm Meaney as Norm’s brother Stan and grandfather have no chemistry with Schneider, whatsoever, making their banter and love feel unearned. **VERDICT: 0/20**

**Music**

Does anyone want to live on this planet, knowing that this movie’s soundtrack exists? There are two types of music unlicensed songs in this movie, and none of them work even to a bare minimum! "Dessert" and "No Time to Lose" are the first type, and they are the unnecessary type of songs in the movie. Seriously, what do they add to the story, or any single character themselves, giving them a vibe or even a reason to exist in the first place? Second, are "Work This Body”, "Geronimo" and "Shut Up + Dance" which add one thing to the picture: how stupid and annoying this film can be, making it sound like a treat, and a delight to absolutely no one! It does not help that the tracks are more focused on creating pop culture references, than even being tracks in the first place! Stephen McKeon, AKA the score composer, you can never make another music score again, you hear me? **VERDICT: 0/20
**To conclude, Norm Of The North (2016) is an actually horrible movie, with the stupidest amount of effort you could possibly imagine! This was the first movie I ever watched that I hated like this, and never wanted to see it again, and I still stand by that opinion! It is so bad, that I would not recommend this to any single person. If you like this movie, I am happy for you, but I have not a clue that people who like this film even exist in the first place! Final Score: 0%
