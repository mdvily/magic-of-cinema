# **Winnie The Pooh (2011)**. In 2011, Disney was on a bad streak. They had produced four animated features, and the first three, in my humble opinion were… not great. Gnomes And Juliet, while its title had a certain… ring to it, was a good enough film with problems. Mars Needs Moms was terrible, with no charm whatsoever and Cars 2 was and still is Pixar’s worst movie. Finally, Winnie The Pooh released as Walt Disney Pictures’ turn. Now, if you recall back on my last review, I did not like its predecessor, The Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh (1977), hailing it as an overrated and pretty bad movie. Now, this film is considered to be the least weak animated film of its release year, but is it really honey in a bottle of garbage, or do we have to wait until 2012? SPOILERS!

# Story

# The story is basically the same thing as the predecessor, only a bit more funny, especially with the knot joke. I might as well start off with the one thing I actually liked about the storytelling: the comedy. This is a pretty funny movie, as the jokes come out of nowhere, that is the point of a comedy: to make unexpected jokes. This film really gets the gist of things, considering how the jokes are perfectly intact with the characters saying them. Now, for what I did not like. First off, the heart is just a missed potential. I feel like if the characters could use the comedy to prove how much they love being around each other, but no. There are no heartfelt moments, which could really help out the story. But my least favorite part of the plot? There is not really much of a plot, mind you. What I really hate is that this is such an unoriginal film. It is literally three shorts. Three shorts rolled up into one, in order to make a movie with some of the worst storytelling I have ever heard of. To the point where there is no storytelling, just unbelievable chaos, of characters meeting each other, wacky hijinks ensuing, without any originality. Why did the stories of Pooh looking for honey, Eeyore getting a new tail, and the Backson, which could have been shorts on their own. The pacing is equally terrible, as we have no transitions to the next short, if you even can call it that. But what hurts me the most? This would have been acceptable, if not was this film officially considered a movie, and not just a bunch of shorts. To conclude, the story sucks, but is a minor improvement. VERDICT: 5/20

# Visuals

# The animation looks great! Along with The Princess And The Frog (2009), Winnie The Pooh shows that hand drawn 2D animation is not dead. The character designs have not even remotely changed, with the sole exception of Christopher Robin, who received a Rescuers: Down Under (1990) makeover. In other words, I do wonder why not the character designs looked far less simplistic. However, the colors are all over the rainbow in the best possible way, and the backgrounds look like how it SHOULD look, when these books to celebrate the 85th year anniversary of the original book, in a movie, for this special occasion. The character animations look so different from the rest of the media,can’t while yes, objects moving in the way they do is lazy, it is the character animations that are not. Especially Eric Goldberg’s character movement of Rabbit. VERDICT: 16/20

# Characters

# As of right now, these characters have spent near 100 years, or as others would say, a century being iconic, and the movie still makes them iconic, but I prefer more complex characters, not one dimensional ones that make the story feel like it goes nowhere. For our main three characters, you have Pooh, who wants to be kind and loves honey a lot, Piglet who is a bit of a coward, who never really changes by the end and Rabbit, who is my favorite, due to him being grumpy, yet caring, resulting in him to be the most well written, and the least of an overused trope. Backson, however, is an F-list villain, as due to the movie insisting that he is real, while he indeed is not, the movie gets confused on a question: what are they doing with him? Eeyore is depressed the whole time, and feels like he belongs in a different movie, due to his nature not fitting well inside of this larger than life franchise. Owl, Kanga and Roo are all so adorable, and make the movie feel lived in, though I wish they were more prominent to the story. Tigger is larger than life, and every time he bounces, he elevates the character animation. Finally, The Narrator is charming, yet he gets overused to not just being a narrator, but a character in the movie. I have no idea why they made this choice, and it was not for the better. To conclude, the characters are perfectly serviceable, only I wish they had more character traits. VERDICT: 10/20.

# Acting

# Jim Cummings is Pooh. Cunning’s portrayal is charming, and really immerses us in Pooh as a character, even when he references his honey! Travis Oates is Piglet, and Piglet’s stuttering and hiccups, making him ever so charming is still there, and a bit better, at that! Tom Kenny is Rabbit, and Rabbit sounds grumpy, yet loving all over again, but Kenny makes his charm feel like the portrayal came from him, and that is a good thing! Huell Howser is Backson, and Howser never feels threatening, or humorous. You would think that he would have something, but no, here we are. The late Bud Luckey is Eeyore. Luckey feels like he has no personality. Yes, that is the point, but the arrow misses the target, once again. Craig Ferguson, Kristen Anderson-Lopez and Wyatt Dean Hall are Owl, Kanga and Roo. The three sound like actual friends, and do something hard: have incredible chemistry with everyone else. Good job, you three! Joachim Kaps and John Cleese as Tigger and the Narrator finally have real personalities, but beneath them, they still have their charm. VERDICT: 16/20

# Music

# The songs feel reminiscent to the music made by the Sherman Brothers, who made the 1977 classic’s music. And I find them just as good as the 1977 movie. We begin with the most charming song and the only one I love to an 10/10, "Winnie the Pooh", the theme song. It feels so charming, full of life and vibrant, like a depressed grandson seeing his grandfather after ten years, and the depression is gone. In other words, the movie has this song to present to you the charm of this world, and it works! “The Winner Song” is super annoying, as it happens every time you find a new tail for Eeyore. They should only have done it once! "So Long" is the ending song where you think you will shed a tear, it really does. But I never did, because it promised to shed so many tears for us, but it just does not! "The Backson Song" presents the Backson as this campy antagonist, but he is not even real, Owl made it up by mistake! Finally, "It's Gonna Be Great" and "The Tummy Song" are the catchy songs, but have no purpose to the story. This can basically be said for any of the songs. VERDICT: 7/20

# To conclude, I thought that Winnie The Pooh (2011) was a completely serviceable movie, even if it was an improvement over the 1977 movie. I never hated this movie, but O have no nostalgia for it, and do not care about its existence, whatsoever. If you love Winnie The Pooh, this is a must watch, but if not, you may skip this. Final Score: 54%
