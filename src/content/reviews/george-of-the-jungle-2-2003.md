# **George Of The Jungle 2 (2003)**. After six years of being a standalone film, George Of The Jungle (1997) had a sequel. Due to low pay, George’s original movie actor Brendan Fraser refused to return to the big screen to play the Tarzan ripoff. Now, Thomas Haden Church as Lyle and John Cleese as Ape were actors who reprised their roles, but with Christopher Showerman as George, it was not the same. And that is certainly true in terms of quality! This movie is actual trash, and a very big disappointment, compared to the surprising love letter, that was the original. The premise? George finally has a family of his own. Said family is his wife, Ursula, and son George Jr. But the family runs into the problem of Ursula’s ex, Lyle teaming up with her mother Beatrice to bring George’s family back into human civilization. But why did I hate it so much? **SPOILERS!**

# **Story**

# The story is basically the exact same movie that the original was. How, you ask? The film so lazily brings callbacks to the original, that I just never get a laugh out of. From George’s not the best vocabulary, to Lyle failing miserably in order to take Ursula back, the movie is desperate. Now, I would not mind, if the movie actually cared about what made the first movie so magical, but instead, with how many there are, the movie instead feels desperate. This also affects the comedy. The movie begs you to laugh so hard at the movie, it just comes out as cringe. I never laughed, chuckled, or even smiled at any of the jokes. But then there is the heart of the movie. The heart is the only element that I found even functional. George’s love for his wife and son is actually something I do relate to. I care about my family, and George cares about his. But even at that, the internal conflict is nonexistent and the external conflict is George saving his family. Guess how much I cared. Not at all. It also does not help how the film tells you everything, and never shows. **VERDICT: 1/20**

# **Visuals**

# This film has one thing I actually like: the color pallet. The film looks very colorful, going all across the color pallet, and it looks beautiful. I love how much effort they put out in, given the 12 million dollar budget. But the 12 million dollar budget is still noticeable. And the showing of San Francisco is noticeably weaker. I never thought that they represented the locations very well. I thought they took such a beautiful premise, and did nothing with it. The same thing can be said with the jungle. I thought that the landscapes did the bare minimum with what they had. I never thought that they did a lot with the locations, and like they just wanted to check the boxes out. The character designs… I mean I never really cared about them. The rendering and character animation really brings it down. The characters look like they were made with special effects for no reason, when they could have used actual animals. But the action? How are the action scenes even action scenes? Every scene is just characters hitting each other, or getting whacked by trees. THAT is the bare minimum. To conclude, The visuals are actually garbage. **VERDICT: 2/20**

# **Characters**

# The characters are all terribly written, and extremely one-dimensional. There is only one character trait to each, and every character is incredibly stupid. George is the stupagonist, I mean protagonist, just wants his son George Jr. to be one with the jungle. George is so one-dimensional, because there is only one character trait to him. The only stupid thing he wants to do is teach his son the ways of the jungle, and is the reason that the story goes nowhere. He never develops, nor helps others become better. Ursula, his girlfriend is even more so. She never does anything, but teach the audience the true meaning of love for your family. That is so unnecessary for this specific movie, because the movie focuses way more on the adventure and trying to be as stupid as the first film. Also, how does the spell not work on her, and why in the end, does George need to kiss the friends of Ursula, to break the spell? George Jr., his son, is an important part of the story, or so I thought. The film never really does anything with him, other than make George go back to save his new family. Shep and Tookie do nothing throughout the film, other than just hang about, and why is Ape at a casino? Did they not rescue him? Beatrice, George’s mother-in-law, is the villain trying to bring George’s family back to civilization. Lyle is another villain, and they just brought him back because they have nothing else to do. And in the end, he gets a wedgie out of the story. What? Mean Lion is the final villain, and he just takes over the jungle because George is not there. They do this all the time, so this is too unoriginal. **VERDICT: 0/20**

# **Acting**

# The acting is a tad bit better than the characters, but it still sucks big time. Christopher Showerman is George. Showerman does exactly nothing with his character, other than make himself the try to be the next Brendan Fraser. It fails miserable, because he sounds nowhere near as funny. I felt like this was his first ever portrayal, and he has a lot to learn. Julie Benz is the new George. Benz is incredibly annoying as her character, and has the worst chemistry with the others, particularly, Angus T. Jones as George Jr. Speaking of which, T. Jones sounds cute, I guess. But that is not enough to make him stand out from the other cute child actor. He does not really stand out, too much. Kevin Greutert as Tookie makes the call, and does the bare minimum with everything else. Christina Pickles is Beatrice Stanhope. Pickled sounds like every other annoying grandmother we have seen, and her body movements could be improved. Thomas Haden Church is Lyle Van De Groot. Now, I like Haden Church as an actor, but this is easily my least favorite role of his. He never really feels so angry that he lost his own fiancé. At least Michael Clarke Duncan as Mean Lion sounds a bit threatening. VERDICT: **2/20**

# Music "George of the Jungle Theme Song” is the theme song, but they changed the lyrics, to match George’s struggles of being a father. I do appreciate the effort, the film never really showcases the importance of these elements. "The Gambler" is easily my least favorite. It sounds like it is too serious to be in a movie like this. The final licensed song is "Lohengrin: Treulich geführt (Bridal Chorus / Wedding March)". The only part of the movie I disliked and did not hate. It sounds a bit romantic, but not appropriate for this movie. Oh yeah, and there is the slapstick sound effects, which sound absolutely stupid. Yes, that is the point, but that is an arrow that should not hit the target. **VERDICT: 2/20**

# **In conclusion**, George Of The Jungle 2 (2003) is actual garbage. Thank god this was not officially released in theaters, but it should not when exist in the first place. Thank god this was not officially released in theaters, but it should not even exist in the first place. I think this could be a good distraction for the children when the mother and father cook dinner, but otherwise, I would never recommend this to anyone. **Final Score: 7%**
