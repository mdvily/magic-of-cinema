Madagascar 3: Europe’s Most Wanted. Madagascar, back in 2005, was a considered great movie, in terms of comedy and heart. The sequel, however, was less well-received, despite being a good film. The third film is not only the least acclaimed, yet the one that is the Madagascar film of the trilogy that is not even considered good. But is it a good movie? SPOILERS!

Story

Right off the bat, the writing of the story is the tiniest bit of an improvement. The story never rips off 101 Dalmatians (1961), like how its predecessor rips off The Lion King (1994). However, the logic is nonexistent. How so? The four African Animals go to one of Europe’s most famous casinos from Africa’s safari, which is too long of a distance. We do not even get to see how that came to be so! Also, if DuBois wanted to saw off Alex’s head in public, how did the crowd not see it happening? Yes, by the time the crew arrives at the circus, things get better, however how much so? Yes it gets a charm from the circus, yet the pacing is too fast, making the victories feeling less earned. VERDICT: 11/20

Visuals

I hated what they did to Monte Carlo, Monaco. It just looks like lines and backgrounds with mountains. That is not what it looks like, due to me having been there, before. It looks stupid, along with the human designs. Yes, they mostly are only in the first act, and yet they are everywhere. However, once we get into the circus, things go from faliurific, to fantastic. The colors, the character designs, the character animations, the action, all of it looks epic. VERDICT: 16/20

Characters

Maybe Alex has a character growth of not running away from his fears, but the other three do not, and just feel like they are here, because they were before. Same thing may apply to the penguins, lemurs and monkeys. They feel like they are here to be funny, which could work better. However, Gia and Stefano have such a respectable desire to save their circus, DuBois has not the most importance of the story, and yet she is terrifying and her motivation to kill Alex makes it personal. However, Vitaly is my favorite character of the entire franchise. The film takes its time to make him from a jerk to a true pure heart. To conclude, the old characters are losing the exact same charm that the new characters are receiving. VERDICT: 13/20

Acting

Ben Stiller, Chris Rock, Jada Pinkett-Smith and David Schwimmer as the four are charming, yet they do the exact same thing that they do with the last two movies. They are charming, yet their fear of the French Animal Control cops is ignored. The penguins and Conrad Vernon as the penguins and monkeys do not do anything great, other than the comedy. Sacha Baron Cohen as Julien is no longer very memorable, just humorous. However, the new cast kills it, and really gives it a charm. Jessica Chastain as Gia and Martin Short as Stefano are charming, yet passionate. Frances McDormand as DuBois sells the terror, yet animal murder passion she needs. However, Brian Cranston as Vitaly goes from loud and boisterous to warm and wonderful. VERDICT: 16/20

Music

What I love about the music is that it does something different from the previous two pictures. It does not even have the main theme, but a new and improved one with French jams. DoBois’s singing is superb, and the firework segment is gorgeously appropriate for the movie. VERDICT: 20/20

This is my worst film I have reviewed yet, however I still enjoy it. It starts off pretty stupidly, yet it gets better, until the end, where it is just masterful. I went from hating, to liking it. Final Score: 73%
