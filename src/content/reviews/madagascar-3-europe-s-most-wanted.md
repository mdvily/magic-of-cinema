Madagascar 3: Europe’s Most Wanted. Madagascar, back in 2005, was a considered great movie, in terms of comedy and heart. The sequel, however, was less well-received, despite being a good film. The third film is not only the least acclaimed, yet the one that is the Madagascar film of the trilogy that is not even considered good. But is it a good movie? SPOILERS!

Story

Right off the bat, the writing of the story is the tiniest bit of an improvement. The story never rips off 101 Dalmatians (1961), like how its predecessor rips off The Lion King (1994). However, the logic is nonexistent. How so? The four African Animals go to one of Europe’s most famous casinos from Africa’s safari, which is too long of a distance. We do not even get to see how that came to be so! Also, if DuBois wanted to saw off Alex’s head in public, how did the crowd not see it happening? Yes, by the time the crew arrives at the circus, things get better, however how much so? Yes it gets a charm from the circus, yet the pacing is too fast, making the victories feeling less earned. VERDICT: 20/20

Acting

Ben Stiller, Chris Rock, Jada Pinkett-Smith and David Schwimmer as the four are charming, yet they do the exact same thing that they do with the last two movies. They are charming, yet their fear of the French Animal Control cops is ignored. The penguins and Conrad Vernon as the penguins and monkeys do not do anything great, other than the comedy. Sacha Baron Cohen as Julien is no longer very memorable, just humorous.
