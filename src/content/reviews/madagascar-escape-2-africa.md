# Madagascar: Escape 2 Africa. At first, I had no idea that sequels existed, and thought its originality was nonexistent, due to it being and looking like the original. I have this to be considered my least talked about movie yet, but maybe I have been too hard on it? SPOILERS!

Story
This is basically The Lion King, but DreamWorks style. Makunga is basically Scar, Zuba is Mufasa, the pride lands is where the 4 live. There is quite a lot of filler, along with Alex’s struggles as a lion. Marty thinking he is the same as everyone else, Melman becoming a doctor, Gloria being attracted to Moto Moto? I hated the third subplot, because WHY DOES MOTO MOTO EXIST?! However, I did not even like the main plot of Alex wanting to be a dancer, however his father wanting him to be an athlete. DID THAT NOT HAPPEN IN HIGH SCHOOL MUSICAL?! I also hate how the logic had no existence. How so, you ask? Zuba should have known Makunga’s motive, due to him being very obvious about it. Now, is it humorous? Yes. Does it have plenty of heart? Indeed. However, I straight-up dislike this story very much. VERDICT: 8/20

# Visuals

# I would say that it ages highly well. The colors are mostly shades of orange and green. The character animations and designs are perfect for the characters’ personalities, and the action of the climax works. However, the character designs for the humans are a massive upgrade, due to them looking like actual people for the movie. This animation for the film, including such adroitly designed the backgrounds make the film look beautiful. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# Alex is a sympathetic character, who probably will be one you want to see win, and follow his dreams. Why? Because the film takes its time to establish his character and necessity in the feature. However, I cannot say the same thing as Marty, Melman and Gloria, who are there to be funny, which does not work, making them just filler. The lemurs, chimps and penguins are also filler, due to them having nothing to do with Alex’s story. At least Zuba, Florence and Makunga are crucial to the plot, but only two of them are but annoying. Also, Nana easily is the greatest part of the movie, due to how she actually is a threat. VERDICT: 10/20

# Acting

# Ben Stiller is Alex. He puts the high energy into this Lion, but can take himself more seriously, making his portrayal grounded. Marty is played by Chris Rock. Rock puts in a solid amount of emotion, kindness and humor. Jada Pinkett-Smith as Gloria has an appropriate maternal voice to the Lion, Zebra and Giraffe. Speaking of which, David Schwimmer is Melman, and while that was a great choice, he doesn’t really lend his voice. That, and he’s more excited than scared, and in this film, Melman was known for being scared. Also, Cedric The Entertainer as Maurice just doesn’t want to be in the picture. He did fine, but he has had better roles. However, Andy Richter as Mort was adorable. Fun fact: the penguins were played by part of the crew of the film. Director Tom McGrath is Skipper, story artist Chris Miller is Kowalski, assistant editor Christopher Knights is Private, and Jeffery Katzenberg is Rico himself. Alec Baldwin was Makunga. I disliked it. It is so humorous, and it is supposed to be creepy. At least he tried. However, if you think of Sacha Baron Cohen, you think of King Julien III from this movie. But Bernie Mac as Zuba is the greatest portrayal of the film. You know why. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# The score is pretty good, fitting the scene nicely. There are multiple beats, such as: The main theme. The theme here is quite delightful, and captures the tone of the scene well. The panic mode track is a jam to listen to, and the highlight of hearing the film. I hated the villain theme in the first movie, and was glad they improved on it. Also, “More Than A Feeling” by Boston was in this movie. It was perfect for the scene where the penguins steal Nana’s truck. VERDICT: 20/20

# This movie is still a good film, due to its comedy, heart, animation, portrayals and music. However, the writing is its weak spot. Still a good movie? Yes. A really good film? Not really. Final Score: 77%
