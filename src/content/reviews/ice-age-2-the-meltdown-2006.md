# **Ice Age 2: The Meltdown (2006)**. As Blue Sky’s first movie, it is easy to see why Ice Age (2002) was a considered blast, and even was a Best Animated Feature nominee. And when it comes to making a sequel, what could possibly go wrong? Well, other than Ice Age sequels then becoming an addiction that cannot be broken, until 15 years after the release of this film, when the studio finally shut down, once and for all, with no return. But that begs the question: was the first sequel the first disaster? SPOILERS!

# Story

# In this movie, after Manny’s (Ray Romano) son and wife’s death, he starts to believe that he is the only mammoth left, until he meets Ellie (Queen Latifah), and at the same time, the group has to stop a flood from killing everyone they know, and destroying their land. While, there is a lot of comedy that is funny, a message on how you are not alone, and a prominent heart, that pours itself out, there is not really much to the story, when I start to think of it. First off, just because Manny lost his family does not make him the only mammoth living to this very day. Second, while the balance between both plots is done very well, both are predictable. The romance plot is done the exact same, as other romcoms, without a real vibe, and for the flood plot, the characters will undeniably live, due to Blue Sky having plot armor all the time. And to make the runtime even longer, they keep putting Scrat (Chris Wedge) scenes, as well as a vulture musical number, that has nothing to do with the story, and just comes out of the blue. VERDICT: 12/20

# Animation

# The animation is an undeniable downgrade from the animation of its predecessor, but does that really make something bad about the film, when it still went from amazing to good? My only real nitpicks are that the backgrounds look like… a place without much detail, and the special effects look dull at times, particularly the water shown in the film. The character designs look far more beautiful, with a lot of detail into the mammals’ fur. I must also emphasize and factor in that in order for this to be reminiscent to a 3D Looney Tunes picture, there is a lot of diligence and effort to the character animation, with my favorite examples being from Scrat and his antics. I also love all of the new creatures, and the creativity of their designs, as well as how the action scenes (especially the rock-breaking segment) go very hard, to the point where for a second, I got invested! To conclude, this is not the greatest animation of all time, but it is still worthy of praise. VERDICT: 14/20

# Characters

# What the characters may lack in depth, they beyond make up for in character development and the pacing of said arcs. For instance, Sid (John Leguizamo) goes from imagining girls, to accepting that the herd is where he is loved. Diego (Denis Leary) learns to face his fear of water, in order to help his herd avoid this catastrophe of flooding. Crash and Eddie (Josh Peck and Seann William Scott) learn to use their brotherly mischief for the greater good, and to help the herd, not to pull pranks on them, and make their lives miserable. Ellie learns to accept her identity that she is a mammoth, and no longer force herself to be like a possum, and accept Manny as an actual future husband. Finally, Manny goes from thinking himself as the only mammoth, to believing that there are plenty more of his kind. Now, Crash and Eddie serve nothing to the story, and are incredibly annoying. Finally, Cretaceous and Maelstrom are creepy villains, and their motive to just eat makes it real scary, even if they have no real personality. The main cast are humorous, I just wish Scrat could have more story purpose, yet it is an improvement, as he learns to be more plot relevant. VERDICT: 16/20

# Acting

# Ray Romano as Manny has both the concern and grumpiness of Manny, and his line deliveries are oh, so good. John Leguizamo as Sid still has his charm and comedy, plus his drama moments are noticeable. Denis Leary as Diego is fearful of water, and his delivery of his lines is actually pretty good, despite what you may think. Christopher “Chris” Wedge is Scrat, and it feels like he takes crazy pills in the best possible way, which makes no sense, but that is part of its charm. Queen Latifah is Ellie, with her comedy and complexity, even if I do not like her dedication as an actress very much. Finally, Crash and Eddie are played by Josh Peck and Seann William Scott, which give them a vibe, even if it is an annoying vibe. VERDICT: 18/20

# Music

# “Attack from Below the Ice” is a terrifying track with beats that come out of nowhere, perfectly adding to the fear. “Manny to the Rescue” and “Scrat to the Rescue” are comedic, and fully embrace how important to the story they are. But if you want scary tracks for the picture, “The Vulture of Doom” and “Ellie Gets Trapped” have you covered. But my favorite score track is “Mammoths”, which was made by John Powell, but it feels with how authentic is is, that this was created by John Williams. My only real musical gripe is, “Food Glorious Food”, which just made a musical number for the sake of making a musical number. VERDICT: 19/20

# To conclude, Ice Age 2: The Meltdown (2006) is Blue Sky’s very first sequel, and it is a sequel done right. A film that perfectly expands the universe, and gives the characters another flaw, that perfectly gives them an arc, even if the film does have flaws, such as an overall vibe I wish it had. If you loved the first Ice Age movie back in 2002, I would highly suggest watching its sequel 4 years later, but if not, you may look for sequels elsewhere. Final Score: 79%
