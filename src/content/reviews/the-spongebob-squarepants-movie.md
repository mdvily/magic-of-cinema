# The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie. In 1999, SpongeBob SquarePants was finally released into our world. The result? Fans loved the franchise so much, that 5 years later, it had a feature film, The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie. It was a considered funny and charming movie. But is it so? SPOILERS!

# Story

# In this story, SpongeBob and Patrick embark on a 6-day quest to Shell City to retrieve King Neptune's stolen crown. They must prove Mr. Krabs innocent of the theft and stop the villainous Plankton from stealing the Krabby Patty secret formula and enslaving Bikini Bottom. Right off the bat, the story has an episodic plot. How so? Defeating Plankton, proving Mr. Krabs innocent, getting back King Neptune’s crown? They all have very different ideas! Now, as I would like to say, The Execution Is More Important Than The Premise. However, the execution, while could be more focused, just like Mr. SquarePants himself, is still… good. However, it is not about the story, but the charm within it. And unlike Madagascar: Escape 2 Africa, the charm personally works for me. I laughed a lot at the comedy, found the pirates in the second act charmingly amusing, and the heart is very prominent to the story, in the best possible way. VERDICT: 14/20

# Animation

# This is a hybrid between animation and life action. First, the live action looks beautiful. Every character has a design that feels so accurate to the show, and yet the animations know they cannot just do the bare minimum, so they use 2D blended with 3D to make this film look good. And it succeeded! The action scenes are quite intense, however the colors are my favorite aspect. The yellow symbolizes the lighthearted tone, blue shows off the massive and gorgeous worldbuilding, and the character development is shown off in the green. VERDICT: 20/20

# Characters

# We begin with SpongeBob SquarePants, himself. He does not take life very seriously, and yet he is charming, and kind, and along with Patrick Star, I love his journey of taking life seriously, while being his humorous self. Princess Mindy and her father, King Neptune, are the perfect catalysts to the story. Plankton is a humorous, yet surprisingly competent antagonist, with a humorous motivation. David Hasselhoff is a likable guide to the two “Goobers”. Squidward is a humorous grouchy neighbor, and Dennis is a terrifying henchman. I just wish Mr. Krabs (A character crucial to the story, and on the good side) was more likable. VERDICT: 18/20

# Acting

# Tom Kenny is SpongeBob SquarePants, and he kills it with the charm and humor, yet stupidity of SquarePants. Bill Fagerbakke as Patrick Star feels so stupid as Star, and yet that is the point. Scarlett Johansson as Princess Mindy is wise, yet caring. David Hasselhoff portrays himself, with kindness and bravery. Mr. Lawrence (Yes, that is his real name, do not bully him) is Plankton, and is humorous enough. Clancy Brown and Rodger Bumpass as Mr. Krabs and Squidward are humorous. In conclusion, the actors could have more range, however they kill it with the comedy. VERDICT: 19/20

# Music

# The score does not try to be powerful or moving, just wacky. To its credit, it succeeded. I just wish it did a bit more. But the same thing cannot be said for the songs. There are only three songs, and yet they are all incredible. “Goofy Goober Rock” is inspired by Twisted Sister’s “I Wanna Rock” shows SpongeBob’s growth, accepting himself as a “goober”, and is crucial both to the story and SquarePants’ growth as a character. “Now That We're Men” shows the problem between SquarePants and Star. They never accept themselves for who they are. I love how “The Goofy Goober Song” represents SpongeBob’s flaw: he does not take life seriously. VERDICT: 19/20

# In conclusion, this movie is just amazing. It starts off good, but works its way up to masterpiece status. With a cohessive story, breathtaking animation, lovable characters, beautiful acting and incredibly catchy music, this is a must-watch for any SpongeBob SquarePants fan. But even if you are not a die-hard enjoyer of the Nick-Toons  you should still check this awesome comedy out. I sure am glad that I did, and these are the reasons why. Final Score: 90%
