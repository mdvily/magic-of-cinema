This is what blockbuster filmmaking is supposed to look like.

## Story

Villeneuve takes the back half of the first novel and turns it into one of the most confident epics in modern memory. The pacing is *patient* in a way that almost no franchise movie dares to be anymore — long stretches of silence, atmosphere, prophecy. It earns every one of its 166 minutes.

## Visuals

Greig Fraser is on another planet. Literally. The black-and-white Giedi Prime sequence alone is worth the ticket — a stylistic flex so audacious it almost feels like cheating. Worms, explosions, sandstorms, scale. The biggest screen you can find. Don't watch this on a phone. Don't be a Neck No.

## Characters

Chani gets the upgrade she deserved. Stilgar steals every scene he's in. Paul becomes someone you're *not* sure you should root for, which is the whole point.

## Acting

Timothée Chalamet finally looks like a movie star. Zendaya does more with a glance than most actors do with a monologue. Austin Butler is unhinged in the best possible way.

## Music

Hans Zimmer keeps doing things with sound that I didn't think were legal.

## Verdict

This is what cinema can do when nobody flinches. Watch it on the biggest screen you can find. **Cinematic Masterpiece.**
