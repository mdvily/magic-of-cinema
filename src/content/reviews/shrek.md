# **Shrek (2001)**. What was once seen as a parody, became a sensation. I just saw it in theaters for its 25th year rerelease. Seriously, I personally cannot think of a soul who does not remember this fantastically iconic classic. Or so is that what this film is? Well, grab your memes and dim the swamp lights and let us find out! SPOILERS!

# Story

# This story is iconic, in a way. Sure, the pacing can be a bit slow, and the comedy is occasionally grossed out a bit too much, but if you get past those flaws, this is a classic. First off, while the story was seen to be predictable, it became the opposite. There are so many things that happen in the film, and you never expect it, because it has not been done all the time. The comedy is just hilarious! Not only is it very quotable, but it feels perfectly in touch with the characters’ personalities. For example, (Shrek: I thought I told you to stay outside!” Donkey: “I am outside!”) works because it presents Shrek as annoyed and while Donkey as innocent and sweet. Perfectly intact with their characters. However, the heart is exceptionally prominent, perfectly getting right the message of true love and what it means. However, my favorite part of the story is the fairy tale tropes, due to the film knowing perfectly when to use them and when to subvert them, helping the plot, incredibly. Overall, I have way more to commend, than to criticize. VERDICT: 20/20

# Animation

# My problem with some animated films of the 2000’s, especially in the early decade, is that it looks so dated. Fortunately, that is an issue this movie swings right past. Yes, the designs could have more detail, but the color pallet is perfect for the film, taking swampy shades of green, indicating that this is a fairy tale, and the beautiful shades of green, for your information. The character animation, even after 25 years looks like it came out last hour, because it makes the characters look so fluid, in terms of movement. Lastly, the action looks so visceral. Not only because it works in the story’s favor, but because it is so easy to follow. This film might show its age soon, but it does not show how it is from 2001, quite yet. VERDICT: 18/20

# Characters

# Shrek, our main titular Ogre is grumpy, because people take one look at him and assume him as a fairytale terrorist. He is not, so it makes you feel for him, all while he has an actual character arc of knowing what true love and friendship means. Donkey is our comedic sidekick. While he is funny, he actually has a powerful purpose to Shrek’s arc, all while having his own on annoying people less. Princess Fiona is the damsel in distress, and she longs for her true love to come save her, making her way more sympathetic. Lord Farquaad, as the movie’s antagonist, and while he is humorous, I just wish he had a better fear factor. I was also surprised on how they gave a dragon personality, making her also want true love, making her a pretty interesting mirror to Fiona. Finally,  the side fairytale characters of the Gingerbread Man, Gepetto, Pinnochio, the bears, the pigs, wolf and blind mice all make the world feel lived in, with their comedic personalities. VERDICT: 19/20

# Acting

# Mike Myers is Shrek, conveying an appropriate amount of dissatisfaction with life and a grumpy, yet kind demeanor, later in the picture. Eddie Murphy is Donkey. Murphy is humorous, conveying a lot of charm and heart into just a talking donkey, with a lot of heart,  albeit an ICONIC talking donkey, with a lot of heart. Cameron Diaz is Princess Fiona. Diaz is very proper, and like a damsel in distress, exactly what the cursed damsel needs. John Lithgow is Lord Maximus Farquaad. Lithgow is charming, and while he is not scary, is that really a part of ’s character in the first place? The fairy tale creatures are given the undying charm of Conrad Vernon, Chris Miller, Cody Cameron, Christopher Knights and Aron Warner. They all have such different personalities, making this perfect world feel lived in. I just have one thing to say to the casting’s happily ever after. VERDICT: 20/20

# Music

# The score is intense, when need be, all while having a real… charm, at the lighthearted scenes. However, my largest praise, is that it really sounds like a fairytale. You get the idea that this film is supposed to feel magical and in a fantasy world, and I love how much the score supports that idea. However, the main tune is “All Star” by Smash Mouth having a tune that is perfect for the story and worldbuilding, particularly, Shrek’s character. VERDICT: 20/20

# To conclude, somebody once told me that this picture was the animated classic of 2001. I told myself, and if this review did not tell you, what will? It is everything I expect from a Disney parody of this particular time period, without ever feeling dated. The movie is so charming and full of life, that it is impossible to not love it, for many people, including myself! Final Score: 95%
