**The Many Adventures Of Winnie The Pooh (1977)**. As a compilation of three previously released shorts: Winnie the Pooh and the Honey Tree (1966), Winnie the Pooh and the Blustery Day (1968), and Winnie the Pooh and Tigger Too (1974), this movie has a 1977 release, being considered as the final film Walt Disney had any involvement in, prior to his death in 1966. Now, as a young boy from 2014-2018, I was an avid fan of the Winnie The Pooh books. I personally have a lot of nostalgia in them. However, what about the movie 37 years before I started reading the books? Well, the reviews are quite positive, but I do find this movie overrated. Now, I know, that sounds wrong on paper, but this is just my opinion and why I dislike it. If you like this movie, I am happy for you, but I do not, and I will tell you why. SPOILERS!

Story

I might as well start off with the one thing I actually liked about the storytelling: the comedy. This is a pretty funny movie, as the jokes come out of nowhere, that is the point of a comedy: to make unexpected jokes. This film really gets the gist of things, considering how the jokes are perfectly intact with the characters saying them. Now, for what I did not like. First off, the heart is just a missed potential. I feel like if the characters could use the comedy to prove how much they love being around each other, but no. There are no heartfelt moments, which could really help out the story. But my least favorite part of the plot? There is not really much of a plot, mind you. What I really hate is that this is such an unoriginal film. It is literally three shorts. Three shorts rolled up into one, in order to make a movie with some of the worst storytelling I have ever heard of.
