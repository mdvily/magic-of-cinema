# **The Many Adventures Of Winnie The Pooh (1977)**. As a compilation of three previously released shorts: Winnie the Pooh and the Honey Tree (1966), Winnie the Pooh and the Blustery Day (1968), and Winnie the Pooh and Tigger Too (1974), this movie has a 1977 release, being considered as the final film Walt Disney had any involvement in, prior to his death in 1966. Now, as a young boy from 2014-2018, I was an avid fan of the Winnie The Pooh books. I personally have a lot of nostalgia in them. However, what about the movie 37 years before I started reading the books? Well, the reviews are quite positive, but I do find this movie overrated. Now, I know, that sounds wrong on paper, but this is just my opinion and why I dislike it. If you like this movie, I am happy for you, but I do not, and I will tell you why. SPOILERS!

# Story

# I might as well start off with the one thing I actually liked about the storytelling: the comedy. This is a pretty funny movie, as the jokes come out of nowhere, that is the point of a comedy: to make unexpected jokes. This film really gets the gist of things, considering how the jokes are perfectly intact with the characters saying them. Now, for what I did not like. First off, the heart is just a missed potential. I feel like if the characters could use the comedy to prove how much they love being around each other, but no. There are no heartfelt moments, which could really help out the story. But my least favorite part of the plot? There is not really much of a plot, mind you. What I really hate is that this is such an unoriginal film. It is literally three shorts. Three shorts rolled up into one, in order to make a movie with some of the worst storytelling I have ever heard of. To the point where there is no storytelling, just unbelievable chaos, of characters meeting each other, wacky hijinks insuing, without any originality. The pacing is equally terrible, as we have no transitions to the next short, if you even can call it that. But what hurts me the most? This would have been acceptable, if not was this film officially considered a movie, and not just a bunch of shorts. I just cannot believe how much I hate this movie’s story! VERDICT: 3/20

# Visuals

# Let us start off with the good of this movie’s look. The action scenes are quite intense, narratively and visually. The character designs look beautiful here. They look like a hybrid between animals and stuffed toys, adding perfectly on to the charm the film could have looked like it did not have, but that is not the case. Finally, the color pallet is all over the rainbow, in the most beautiful and lovely way you can get from Disney. Now, on to the criticisms I have for the movie. While the color pallet looks good, the xeroxing looks noticeable. You really see how the film looks like it had very little to nothing to work with. The lighting and rendering looks weak, as you never start to feel like this is a world these characters live in. Finally, the character animation looks so stiff and like it does the bare minimum to make the characters move properly, even if the film was released almost 50 years ago! VERDICT: 10/20

# Characters

# Pooh is our protagonist. Now, Pooh is very charming, but you never get the feeling like he has any character struggles, and the script never challenges this character, giving him any character arc. Piglet is terrified of everything for no reason. I bring this up, as this is his only character trait. Rabbit is the opposite and my favorite character, due to the script making him complex in this way: he still cares for his friends, but can get mad at them. Eeyore and Owl are near my least favorite, because at least they have character traits, but one is welcoming, the other is depressed. We get this a lot, making these so much, that it makes them feel so interchangeable with any other character with said traits. Kanga and Roo are supportive, though I wish we saw them more in the picture. Christopher Robin is my least favorite, because he has no character traits whatsoever, yet the film insists he is a character in this picture. At least Tigger is full of energy, and is a delight to watch. VERDICT: 8/20

# Acting

# Sterling Holloway is Pooh. Holloway does deliver a good portrayal of Pooh, as you really buy Pooh as a charming, if a bit naive bear. John Fiedler as Piglet is pretty cute, I just wish that he delivered his stuttering lines with more fear that his character has. Sebastian Cabot is the narrator. You would think that a narrator would have a powerful, yet soothing voice, but Cabot just does not have that. Paul Winchell portrays the tigger of the movie, Tigger. Winchell feels just like Tigger, and is so full of life. Ralph Wright is Eeyore. Wright brings the depressed donkey to life with the bare minimum. Yes, that is the point, but it an arrow that does not hit the target for this supposed to be full of life movie. Junius Matthews is Rabbit. Matthews has such an attitude that I love. Barbara Luddy and Clint Howard are the mother Kanga and the child Roo. Luddy and Howard bring so much amazing chemistry with each other, I thought they were actually related! Hal Smith is Owl. Smith is so grumpy, in a way that feels very out of Owl’s character. Bruce Reitherman, one of the two actors who is alive today, is Christopher Robin. Reitherman is so full of life and has such amazing chemistry with everyone else. Howard Morris is the Groundhog, and has just energy that does not reflect well on his character’s grumpiness. To conclude, the actors do a fine job, I just wish that the castings were better. VERDICT: 11/20

# Music

# "Winnie the Pooh", "Up, Down, and Touch the Ground", "Hip-Hip-Hooray!" and "Rumbly in My Tumbly" feel like Pooh sung them, adding on to its charm. The problem is? They did not even need to be in the movie, in the first place. They just were for some reason, I do not know why. "The Rain, Rain, Rain Came Down, Down, Down", "Little Black Rain Cloud" and "A Rather Blustery Day" are more crucial to the story. I just wish they were not in the movie, as they did not need these seven songs. Especially, "Heffalumps and Woozles". But if you ask me, the entire scene had no purpose in the movie. It feels so uncomfortable, when the movie is supposed to feel like a bedtime story. The only song necessary to the story is "The Wonderful Thing About Tiggers", which establishes Tigger’s character really well. However, the main tracks stop me from hating the movie, too much. At least they feel so charming and comforting. VERDICT: 7/20

# To conclude, I really dislike this movie. Now, I wish that it were executed well, so I could praise it, I really do, but this is what we get from Disney’s 22nd animated feature, and it was for another. If you really like Winnie The Pooh, you should check this out, but if not, then maybe, you should look elsewhere, as this film was just not for me. Final Score: 39%
